Short title\nThis Act may be cited as the Promoting Rural Exports Act of 2025 .\n#### 2. Findings\nCongress finds the following:\n**(1)**\nRural businesses are often located far from information clusters and major transportation corridors.\n**(2)**\nBecause of their location, rural businesses face higher barriers to accessing international markets.\n**(3)**\nA dedicated National Rural Export Center within the United States and Foreign Commercial Service, and regional rural export centers, providing business- and product-specific support can help companies in the United States looking to export their products.\n#### 3. and\n**(ii)**\nto such offices not located in major metropolitan areas.\n**(C) Location of staff**\nAny researcher or staff directly supporting the operation of the National Rural Export Center shall be primarily based at the National Rural Export Center.\n##### (c) Establishment of regional rural export centers\n**(1) In general**\nNot later than 1 year after the date of the enactment of this Act, the Assistant Secretary shall establish not more than 9 regional rural export centers for the purposes of providing businesses located in rural areas within their regions with resources to help those businesses export their products.\n**(2) Subordination**\nThe programs, activities, and expenditures of the regional rural export centers shall be subordinate to and delegated by the National Rural Export Center.\n##### (d) Export Center operations\n**(1) In general**\nThe National Rural Export Center and regional rural export centers shall\u2014\n**(A)**\nprovide in-depth, customized, and actionable market research services that\u2014\n**(i)**\na business may opt into based on need; and\n**(ii)**\nare\u2014\n**(I)**\nfocused on actionable and measurable results for a business;\n**(II)**\nbusiness- and product-specific;\n**(III)**\nbased on high-quality data, including data from international trade association subscription databases;
