Preservation of State, local, and Tribal authorities with respect to civil unmanned aircraft systems\n##### (a) Findings; sense of Congress\n**(1) Findings**\nCongress finds the following:\n**(A)**\nUsing its constitutional authority to regulate commerce among the States, Congress granted the Federal Government authority over all of the navigable airspace in the United States in order to foster air commerce.\n**(B)**\nWhile the regulation of the navigable airspace is within the Federal Government's domain, the Supreme Court recognized in United States v. and\n**(C)**\nthe Federal Government lacks the authority to intrude upon a State\u2019s sovereign right to issue reasonable time, manner, and place restrictions on the operation of unmanned aircraft systems operating within the immediate reaches of airspace.\n##### (b) Requirements related to regulations and standards\n**(1) In general**\nIn prescribing regulations or standards related to civil unmanned aircraft systems, the following shall apply:\n**(A)**\nThe Administrator shall not authorize the operation of a civil unmanned aircraft in the immediate reaches of airspace above property without permission of the property owner.\n**(B)**\nSubject to paragraph (2), in the case of a structure that exceeds 200 feet above ground level, the Administrator shall not authorize the operation of a civil unmanned aircraft\u2014\n**(i)**\nwithin 50 feet of the top of such structure; or\n**(ii)**\nwithin 200 feet laterally of such structure or inside the property line of such structure\u2019s owner, whichever is closer to such structure.\n**(C)**\nThe Administrator shall not authorize the physical contact of a civil unmanned aircraft, including such aircraft\u2019s take-off or landing, with a structure that exceeds 200 feet above ground level without permission of the structure\u2019s owner.\n**(D)**\nThe Administrator shall ensure that the authority of a State, local, or Tribal government to issue reasonable restrictions on the time, manner, and place of operation of a civil unmanned aircraft system that is operated below 200 feet above ground level is not preempted.\n**(2) Exception**\nThe limitation on the operation of a civil unmanned aircraft under paragraph (1)(B) shall not apply if\u2014\n**(A)**\nthe operator of such aircraft has the permission of the structure\u2019s owner;\n**(B)**\nsuch aircraft is being operated directly within or above an authorized public right of way; or\n**(C)**\nsuch aircraft is being operated on an authorized commercial route designated under subsection (c).\n**(3) Reasonable restrictions**\nFor purposes of paragraph (1)(D), reasonable restrictions on the time, manner, and place of operation of a civil unmanned aircraft system include the following:\n**(A)**\nSpecifying limitations on speed of flight over specified areas.\n**(B)**\nProhibitions or limitations on operations in the vicinity of schools, parks, roadways, bridges, moving locations, or other public or private property.\n**(C)**\nRestrictions on operations at certain times of the day or week or on specific occasions such as parades or sporting events, including sporting events that do not remain in one location.\n**(D)**\nProhibitions on careless or reckless operations, including operations while the operator is under the influence of alcohol or drugs.\n**(E)**\nOther prohibitions that protect public safety, personal privacy, or property rights, or that manage land use or restrict noise pollution.\n##### (c) Designation of authorized commercial routes\n**(1) In general**\nFor purposes of subsection (b)(2)(C), not later than 18 months after the date of enactment of this Act, the Administrator shall establish a process for the designation of routes as authorized commercial routes.
