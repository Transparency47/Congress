Sense of Congress\nIt is the sense of Congress that\u2014\n**(1)**\nthe strategic delivery of foreign assistance is critical to supporting the United States\u2019 interests around the world;\n**(2)**\nforeign assistance furthers United States' national security priorities, advances United States' values, and ensures the United States is the partner of choice for areas such as defense procurement and economic investment in cutting edge scientific research;\n**(3)**\nthe administration of foreign assistance programming should be\u2014\n**(A)**\nmanaged by a qualified, competent Department of State official; and\n**(B)**\nsubject to strict congressional oversight pursuant to its constitutionally authorized power of the purse; and\n**(4)**\nsection 1413 of the Foreign Affairs Reform and Restructuring Act of 1998 (Division G of Public Law 105\u2013277 ; 22 U.S.C. 6563 ) codified into law the status of the United States Agency for International Development as an independent establishment outside of the Department of State.\n#### 3. (B) Personnel decision The term personnel decision means any decision relating to promotions, transfers, demotions, or dismissals. (2) Establishment There is established in the Department of State, a Director of Foreign Assistance (referred to in this subsection as the Director ), who\u2014 (A) shall report directly to the Deputy Secretary of State for Management and Resources; and (B) should not hold another position in the United States Government while holding the position of Director. (3) Duties The Director shall\u2014 (A) optimize the impact of foreign assistance by\u2014 (i) strategically aligning foreign assistance resources to the Secretary of State\u2019s overarching foreign policy goals; (ii) measuring effectiveness; and (iii) promoting evidence-based policies, including budget formulation, justification, and execution mechanisms; (B) serve as a resource for the Secretary of State, the Deputy Secretary of State, the Under Secretaries of State, the Administrator of the United States Agency for International Development, and the heads of other Federal departments and agencies that implement United States foreign assistance programs to support the development of policies, plans, and programs to achieve national security, foreign policy, and development goals; (C) perform results-oriented functions related to United States foreign assistance, including\u2014 (i) leading an integrated budget, strategic planning, and performance process as part of integrated interagency program planning to amplify foreign assistance; (ii) promoting monitoring and evaluation; (iii) increasing transparency; (iv) facilitating data analysis, information management, and reporting; and (v) developing foreign assistance policies through research and partnerships; (D) support interagency collaboration on foreign assistance programs and policies of other Federal agencies and entities, including the Millennium Challenge Corporation, the United States International Development Finance Corporation, the Department of the Treasury, the United States Trade and Development Agency, the Export-Import Bank of the United States, and the Peace Corps; (E) create and direct, as part of integrated interagency program planning to amplify foreign assistance, consolidated policy, strategic, and program plans, the operational budget, program evaluation and results reporting from bureaus and offices within the Department of State and the United States Agency for International Development.
