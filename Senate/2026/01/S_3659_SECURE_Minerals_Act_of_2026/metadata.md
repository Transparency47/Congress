and\n**(E)**\nreviewing and approving all changes to the organization of the Reserve;\n**(3)**\nappoint and fix, by a vote of not fewer than 5 of the 7 members of the Board, and without regard to the provisions of chapter 51 and subchapter III of chapter 53 of title 5, United States Code, the compensation and adjustments to compensation of all personnel of the Reserve, subject to the condition that in appointing and fixing any compensation or adjustments to compensation under this paragraph, the Board shall\u2014\n**(A)**\nconsult with, and seek to maintain comparability with, other comparable Federal personnel, as the Board may determine to be appropriate;\n**(B)**\nconsult with the Office of Personnel Management; and\n**(6)**\nestablish the risk and audit committees described in section 401.\nII\nDuties and authorities of the Reserve\n#### 201. General authorities\nTo the extent necessary to develop, operate, or maintain the Reserve, the Reserve may\u2014\n**(1)**\nissue rules, regulations, or orders;\n**(2)**\nacquire by purchase land or interests in land for the location of storage and related facilities;\n**(3)**\nconstruct, purchase, lease, or otherwise acquire storage and related facilities;\n**(4)**\nuse, lease, maintain, sell, or otherwise dispose of land or interests in land, or of storage and related facilities acquired under this Act, under such terms and conditions as the Board considers necessary or appropriate;\n**(5)**\nacquire, subject to the requirements of this Act, by purchase, exchange, or otherwise, critical minerals or materials for storage;\n**(6)**\nstore critical minerals or materials in storage facilities owned and controlled by the United States or in storage facilities owned by authorized intermediaries if the Reserve has sufficient contractual certainty of access to the critical minerals and materials and those facilities are subject to audit by the United States;\n**(7)**\nexecute contracts with private entities for the storage of critical minerals and materials at storage facilities owned by private entities if the Reserve has sufficient contractual certainty of access to those critical minerals and materials and those facilities are subject to audit by the United States;\n**(8)**\npartner with private sector, academia, and Federal agencies to further the purposes of the Reserve, including to advance the development and commercialization of responsible reuse and recycling processes for critical minerals and materials; and\n**(9)**\nexecute any contracts necessary to develop, operate, or maintain the Reserve.\n#### 202. Identification of eligible critical minerals and materials\n##### (a) Eligible critical minerals and materials list\nSubject to subsections (b), (c), and (d), the Reserve, in consultation with the heads of Federal departments and agencies described in section 203(6), shall establish, and thereafter maintain, a list of critical minerals and materials eligible for financing or acquisition support described in section 206.\n##### (b) Requirements\n**(1) Establishment**\nA mineral or material may only be included on the list of eligible critical minerals and materials established under subsection (a) if\u2014\n**(A)**\nthe mineral or material is\u2014\n**(i)**\nincluded on the list of critical minerals published by the United States Geological Survey pursuant to section 7002(c) of the Energy Act of 2020 ( 30 U.S.C. 1606(c) );\n**(ii)**\nincluded on the list of critical materials published by the Department of Energy pursuant to section 7002(a) of the Energy Act of 2020 ( 30 U.S.C. 1606(a) ); or\n**(iii)**\na material of interest designated by the Director of the Defense Logistics Agency; and\n**(B)**\nthe Reserve determines that the mineral or material\u2014\n**(i)**\nis a non-fuel mineral or material;\n**(ii)**\nhas a vulnerable or highly concentrated supply chain; and\n**(iii)**\nis necessary\u2014\n**(I)**\nfor the national defense and national security requirements of the United States;\n**(II)**\nfor the energy infrastructure of the United States, including\u2014\n**(aa)**\npipelines;\n**(bb)**\nrefining capacity;\n**(cc)**\nelectrical power generation, storage, transmission, and distribution;\n**(dd)**\nrenewable energy production; and\n**(ee)**\nenergy storage;\n**(III)**\nto support domestic manufacturing, agriculture, housing, telecommunications, health care, or transportation and transportation infrastructure; or\n**(IV)**\nfor the economic security of the United States.\n**(2) Modifications**\n**(A) Additions**\nAfter the list of eligible critical minerals and materials is established under paragraph (1), the Reserve may add a mineral or material to the list of eligible critical minerals and materials maintained under subsection (a) if, after the date that the most recent list is published under subsection (e)(1)\u2014\n**(i)**\nthe mineral or material\u2014\n**(I)**\nis included on the most recently published list of critical minerals published by the United States Geological Survey pursuant to section 7002(c) of the Energy Act of 2020 ( 30 U.S.C. 1606(c) );\n**(II)**\nis included on the most recently published list of critical materials published by the Department of Energy pursuant to section 7002(a) of the Energy Act of 2020 ( 30 U.S.C. 1606(a) ); or\n**(III)**\na material of interest designated by the Director of the Defense Logistics Agency; and\n**(ii)**\nthe Reserve determines that the mineral or material meets the requirements described in paragraph (1)(B).\n**(B) Removals**\nAfter the list of eligible critical minerals and materials is established under paragraph (1), the Reserve shall remove a mineral or material from the list of eligible critical minerals and materials maintained under subsection (a) if\u2014\n**(i)**\nthe mineral or material, as of the date on which the list is published under subsection (e)(1)\u2014\n**(I)**\nhas not been included on a list of critical minerals published by the United States Geological Survey pursuant to section 7002(c) of the Energy Act of 2020 ( 30 U.S.C. 1606(c) ) for a period of at least 3 years;\n**(II)**\nhas not been included on a list of critical materials published by the Department of Energy pursuant to section 7002(a) of the Energy Act of 2020 ( 30 U.S.C. 1606(a) ) for a period of at least 3 years; or\n**(III)**\nhas not been designated as a material of interest by the Director of the Defense Logistics Agency for a period of at least 3 years; or\n**(ii)**\nthe Reserve determines that the mineral or material no longer meets the requirements described in paragraph (1)(B).\n##### (c) Considerations\nIn establishing and maintaining the list of eligible critical minerals and materials under subsection (a), the Reserve shall consider\u2014\n**(1)**\nthe results of any assessments conducted under sections 204 and 205;\n**(2)**\nthe existing market infrastructure and financial environment for a given critical mineral or material, especially domestically or in partner countries;\n**(3)**\nthe substitutability of, and projected demand for, a given critical mineral or material; and\n**(4)**\nother information the Reserve determines necessary to achieve the purposes of the Reserve.\n##### (d) Exclusions\nA mineral or material may not be included on the list of eligible critical minerals and materials established and maintained under subsection (a) if the mineral or material is\u2014\n**(1)**\noil, oil shale, natural gas, coal, or uranium;\n**(2)**\nwater, ice, or snow; or\n**(3)**\na common variety, as determined by the Board, of sand, gravel, stone, pumice, cinders, or clay.\n##### (e) Update\n**(1) In general**\nThe Reserve shall publish and update not less frequently than annually the list of eligible critical minerals and materials established and maintained under subsection (a).\n**(2) Requirement**\nIn carrying out paragraph (1), the Reserve shall separately publish a list of minerals and materials\u2014\n**(A)**\npreviously included on a list published under paragraph (1) but were removed within the previous 3 years; and\n**(B)**\nnot included in the list published under paragraph (1) but with respect to which the Reserve has an active position, contract, or transaction.\n#### 203. Data collection\nThere is established within the Reserve a division, to be known as the Division of Data Collection , which, to the extent practicable, shall\u2014\n**(1)**\nbe led by a Director selected by the Board;\n**(2)**\ndevelop and maintain a proprietary dataset sufficient to ensure the thorough analysis of global critical minerals and materials markets;\n**(3)**\ncollect and maintain sufficient datasets, including data comprising global, domestic, and partner country markets and, to the extent possible, data derived from individual critical mineral and material projects, to inform and estimate\u2014\n**(A)**\nproduction, extraction, infrastructure, repurposing, and recycling costs for critical minerals and materials supply chains;\n**(B)**\ncollection and recycling rates for critical minerals and materials in domestic and partner country markets; and\n**(C)**\nthe forecast of supply and demand of critical minerals and materials within domestic and partner country markets;\n**(4)**\ncollect and maintain\u2014\n**(A)**\nactual transaction price data for critical minerals and materials in the global market, including geographic data; and\n**(B)**\nany other datasets necessary to effectuate such purpose, including modeled transaction data and datasets produced by or derivative of datasets produced by the People's Republic of China;\n**(5)**\nusing the most current data collected under paragraphs (3) and (4), support the activities described in sections 204 and 206;\n**(6)**\nconsult with relevant heads of Federal departments and agencies, including\u2014\n**(A)**\nthe Secretary of Agriculture;\n**(B)**\nthe Secretary of Commerce;\n**(C)**\nthe Secretary of Defense;\n**(D)**\nthe Secretary of Energy;\n**(E)**\nthe Secretary of the Interior;\n**(F)**\nthe Secretary of State;\n**(G)**\nthe Secretary of the Treasury;\n**(H)**\nthe Chief Executive Officer of the United States International Development Finance Corporation;\n**(I)**\nthe Director of the Central Intelligence Agency;\n**(J)**\nthe Director of the United States Geological Survey;\n**(K)**\nthe President of the Export-Import Bank of the United States; and\n**(8)**\nto the extent practicable, carry out the responsibilities of this section using existing government data and information.\n#### 204. Critical mineral and material market risk and vulnerability assessment\n##### (a) Establishment\nThere is established within the Reserve a division, to be known as the Division of Risk and Vulnerability Evaluation , which shall\u2014\n**(1)**\nbe led by a Director selected by the Board;\n**(2)**\ndevelop or, to the extent practicable, use existing sophisticated models to evaluate threats and risks in critical mineral and material markets across United States industrial sectors, including defense, energy, agriculture, transportation, health, and emerging technology;\n**(3)**\nmaintain a comprehensive database of critical mineral and material price movements, supply chain vulnerabilities, production and processing capacities, and consumption patterns;\n**(4)**\nidentify critical dependencies in critical mineral and material markets that could threaten national security or economic stability;\n**(5)**\nassess the potential for geopolitical events, natural disasters, technological disruptions, or market failures to impact commodity markets;\n**(6)**\ndevelop and implement methodologies for modeling the impact of various critical mineral or material shocks on the United States economy;\n**(7)**\nassess vulnerabilities, including price spikes, supply disruptions, transportation failures, export controls, and financial market disturbances;\n**(8)**\nmodel the cross-sectoral impacts of critical mineral or material price or supply shocks, including effects on inflation, employment, government finances, and consumer welfare;\n**(9)**\nassess the specific impact of critical mineral or material disruptions on infrastructure, national security assets, and essential services; and\n**(10)**\nto the extent practicable, carry out the responsibilities of this section using existing government data and information.\n##### (b) Mandatory risk assessment\n**(1) In general**\nThe Reserve shall conduct and submit to the appropriate congressional committees, the President, and the heads of Federal departments and agencies listed in section 203(6) a biennial comprehensive risk and vulnerability assessment for critical minerals and materials, which shall include\u2014\n**(A)**\nidentification of specific threats to stable supply and prices;\n**(B)**\nan analysis of current market conditions, including geographic and ownership concentration of suppliers, transportation bottlenecks, and financial vulnerabilities;\n**(C)**\nan evaluation of substitution possibilities and technological alternatives; and\n**(D)**\nrecommendations for risk mitigation strategies.\n**(2) Form of assessment**\n**(A) In general**\nEach assessment required by paragraph (1)\u2014\n**(i)**\nshall be submitted in unclassified form; but\n**(ii)**\nmay include a classified annex.\n**(B) Requirement**\nAny assessments required by paragraph (1) that include a classified annex shall include an unclassified summary.\n#### 205. Production standards\nThere is established within the Reserve a division, to be known as the Division of Production Standards , which shall\u2014\n**(1)**\nbe led by a Director selected by the Board;\n**(2)**\ndevelop methodologies for evaluating relative risk in global environmental and labor standards and practices for the production, extraction, processing, reuse, repurposing, and recycling of critical minerals and materials, including transparency, traceability, and forced labor risk, which may include incorporating existing research;\n**(3)**\nconduct periodic risk-based assessments of environmental and labor standards and practices for the production, extraction, processing, reuse, repurposing, and recycling of critical minerals and materials in foreign countries producing critical minerals and materials, and, to the extent practicable, significant production projects; and\n**(4)**\npublish an annual report summarizing the methodologies used and describing the results of the most recent assessments conducted under paragraph (3) for each foreign country and significant production project, and, to the extent practicable, mitigation measures used in transactions and loans made by the Reserve, without identifying proprietary or sensitive commercial information.\n#### 206. Financing and acquisition of critical minerals or materials\n##### (a) Authority\n**(1) In general**\nThe Reserve may deploy financing and acquisition tools as described in subsection (b) to achieve the purposes of the Reserve, subject to the condition that the Reserve may not deploy such tools to benefit a foreign entity of concern.\n**(2) Considerations**\nIn carrying out this section, the Reserve shall consider\u2014\n**(A)**\nthe results of the assessments described in section 205(3);\n**(B)**\nthe ability of the Reserve to efficiently achieve the purposes of the Reserve with limited resources;\n**(C)**\ndiversification across critical minerals and materials;\n**(D)**\nnon-Reserve investments and market developments regarding a specific critical mineral or material;\n**(E)**\nwith respect to deploying financing and acquisition tools with a specific producer or processor, the management, financial condition, and ability of the producer or processor to fulfill any contractual obligations; and\n**(F)**\nother factors the Reserve determines valuable to achieving the purposes of the Reserve over an extended period of time.\n**(3) Federal Government Investments**\nThe Reserve shall, to the maximum extent practicable in carrying out this section, consult, coordinate, and leverage existing Federal Government investments, including by\u2014\n**(A)**\nthe Export-Import Bank of the United States;\n**(B)**\nthe United States International Development Finance Corporation;\n**(C)**\nthe Department of Energy, pursuant to title XVII of the Energy Policy Act of 2005 ( 42 U.S.C. 16511 et seq. );\n**(D)**\nthe Office of Strategic Capital of the Department of Defense; and\n**(E)**\napplicable execution offices of the Department of Defense for contract actions carried out under title III of the Defense Production Act of 1950 ( 50 U.S.C. 4531 et seq. ).\n##### (b) Means of support\nThe financing and acquisition tools referred to in subsection (a) include the following:\n**(1) Loans to authorized intermediaries**\n**(A) Loan program authorized**\nThe Reserve may make loans to authorized intermediaries who may use those funds to enter into financing and purchasing agreements with producers and processors of critical minerals or materials.\n**(B) Loan conditions**\n**(i) In general**\nIn making loans under subparagraph (A), the Reserve shall establish such terms and conditions as the Reserve determines appropriate to achieve the purposes of the Reserve.\n**(ii) Adjustment of loan terms**\nThe Reserve and an authorized intermediary may adjust loan terms under a loan issued under subparagraph (A) if the Reserve and that authorized intermediary agree to the adjustment.\n**(iii) Preferential terms for certain loans**\nIn making loans under subparagraph (A), the Reserve\u2014\n**(I)**\nmay provide preferential loan terms\u2014\n**(aa)**\nwhich may include an interest rate equal to the Federal funds rate, to an authorized intermediary that will use the loan to enter into financing and purchasing agreements with producers or processors of critical minerals or materials; and\n**(bb)**\nto authorized intermediaries that will use the loan to enter into financing and purchasing agreements with producers or processors of critical minerals or materials in partner countries, in such manner as the Reserve determines appropriate; and\n**(II)**\nshall\u2014\n**(aa)**\nconsult with the heads of Federal departments and agencies described in section 203(5) with respect to the loan terms described in subclause (I)(aa); and\n**(bb)**\nensure that, under the terms of such loans, authorized intermediaries shall, to the maximum extent practicable, give priority to United States suppliers of critical minerals and materials and preference to the United States supply chain.\n**(C) Proposal solicitation**\nTo be eligible to receive a loan under subparagraph (A), an authorized intermediary shall submit to the Reserve an application at such time, in such manner, and containing such information as the Reserve may require, including the proposed financing or purchasing agreements described in that subparagraph.\n**(D) Uncured default**\n**(i) In general**\nIf an authorized intermediary fails to make a required repayment on a loan under subparagraph (A) for a 90-day period, the Reserve may\u2014\n**(I)**\nrecoup the amount of that loan by taking possession of the critical mineral and material inventories of the authorized intermediary and any other contractual rights of the authorized intermediary to receive critical minerals or materials from suppliers;\n**(II)**\nrevoke the participation with the Reserve of the authorized intermediary;\n**(III)**\nsubject to clause (ii), appoint itself as conservator or receiver of the authorized intermediary;\n**(IV)**\nobtain a lien on the assets of the intermediary pursuant to section 102(c)(5)(J); and\n**(V)**\nadjust the loan terms pursuant to subparagraph (B)(ii).\n**(ii) Authorities under conservator or receivership**\nIf the Reserve appoints itself a conservator or receiver of an authorized intermediary under clause (i)(II), the Reserve shall have the same authorities with respect to the authorized intermediary that the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation has with respect to an institution for which the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation has appointed itself as conservator or receiver under the Federal Deposit Insurance Act ( 12 U.S.C. 1811 et seq. ).\n**(iii) Treatment of bankruptcy**\nAn authorized intermediary for which the Reserve has appointed itself a conservator or a receiver under clause (i)(II) may not be placed into bankruptcy under title 11, United States Code, during that conservatorship or receivership, and any bankruptcy process under title 11, United States Code, that is in effect when the appointment occurs shall be terminated.\n**(2) Acquisitions**\n**(A)**\nAcquisition through solicitation and direct contracting with private counterparties.\n**(B)**\nAcquisition through physically cleared financial instruments, such as futures contracts through intermediaries, including financial exchanges.\n**(C)**\nAcquisition through options contracts directly or through intermediaries, including financial exchanges.\n**(3) Non-recourse lending**\nNon-recourse lending to projects secured by a portion of the expected production of the project.\n**(4) Other transactions**\nOther financing and acquisition transactions, including contract for differences, advance or milestone payments, advanced market commitments, and minority, non-controlling equity investment, as determined by the Board as necessary to fulfill the purposes of the Reserve, except that equity investment shall only be used with\u2014\n**(A)**\na written justification describing how other financing and acquisition tools in this section are not sufficient; and\n**(B)**\na written explanation of the intended exit strategy for the equity investment.\n##### (c) Partner co-Investment\n**(1) In general**\nA partner country may, if approved by the Reserve, make capital contributions of at least $100,000,000 to the Reserve for purposes of financing or acquisition under subsection (b).\n**(2) Minimum amount**\nThe Reserve shall annually adjust the amount in paragraph (1) by the percentage increase in the Personal Consumption Expenditures Price Index of the Bureau of Economic Analysis of the Department of Commerce, rounded to the nearest $1,000,000.\n**(3) Treatment of capital contributions**\nThe Reserve\u2014\n**(A)**\nshall maintain separate accounts for the capital contributions of each partner country that provides such contributions under paragraph (1);\n**(B)**\nshall not commingle the capital contributions of any partner country with any other partner country or the funds of the Reserve;\n**(C)**\nmay return such capital contributions to the partner country at any time, without obligation or penalty, or under such other terms and conditions as agreed to by the Reserve and that partner country; and\n**(D)**\nmay not guarantee the repayment of such capital contributions to a partner country.\n**(4) Loans made with partner co-investment funds**\nFinancing and acquisitions made under subsection (b) with capital contributions under paragraph (1) shall be made in the same manner as financing and acquisitions made under subsection (b) with funds of the Reserve.\n**(5) Restriction**\nThe Reserve may not approve a partner country under paragraph (1) unless the partner country certifies that the capital contributions being made are coming from funds of the partner country and not from funds of a foreign entity of concern or a covered country.\n##### (d) International advisory council of partners\n**(1) In general**\nThe Reserve may establish an International Advisory Council of Partners comprising\u2014\n**(A)**\nthe Vice-chairperson, who shall be the head of the council; and\n**(B)**\n1 representative from each partner country that makes a capital contribution under subsection (c)(1).\n**(2) Consultation**\nThe International Advisory Council of Partners shall, at the request of the Reserve, advise the Reserve on financing and acquisitions made with capital contributions under subsection (c)(1).\n**(3) Applicability of FACA**\nChapter 10 of title 5, United States Code (commonly known as the Federal Advisory Committee Act ), shall not apply to the International Advisory Council of Partners.\n#### 207. Sale of critical minerals or materials\n##### (a) Sale\nThe Reserve may sell critical minerals or materials stored in the Reserve in accordance with the purposes of the Reserve and this section.\n##### (b) Sale of critical minerals or materials\nThe Reserve may sell a critical mineral or material stored in the Reserve if the Board determines that\u2014\n**(1)**\na supply shortage or potential supply shortage of that critical mineral or material threatens\u2014\n**(A)**\nthe national or economic security of the United States; or\n**(B)**\nprice stability in the value chain of that critical mineral or material; or\n**(2)**\nthe sale is otherwise necessary to support the purposes of the Reserve.\n##### (c) Sale of non-Critical minerals or materials\n**(1) In general**\nThe Reserve may sell a mineral or material stored in the Reserve that, as of the date of sale, is no longer included on the list of critical minerals and materials established by the Reserve under section 202(a) if the Board determines that\u2014\n**(A)**\nthe mineral or material is unlikely to be imminently re-added to the list of critical minerals and materials established by the Reserve under section 202(a);\n**(B)**\nthe mineral or material is available in sufficient supply or is no longer necessary in large quantities for economic or national security purposes;\n**(C)**\na supply shortage or potential supply shortage of that mineral or material threatens\u2014\n**(i)**\nthe national or economic security of the United States; or\n**(ii)**\nprice stability in the value chain of that mineral or material; or\n**(D)**\nthe sale is otherwise necessary to support the purposes of the Reserve.\n**(2) Use for research purposes**\nIf the Board determines that a mineral or material stored in the Reserve that is no longer included on the list of critical minerals and materials established by the Reserve under section 202(a) does not have a substantial market value, the Board may enter into a contract for the transfer and use for research purposes of that mineral or material with\u2014\n**(A)**\nFederal departments and agencies;\n**(B)**\nState governments;\n**(C)**\nNational Laboratories (as defined in section 2 of the Energy Policy Act of 2005 ( 42 U.S.C. 15801 )); and\n**(D)**\ninstitutions of higher education (as defined in section 101(a) of the Higher Education Act of 1965 ( 20 U.S.C. 1001(a) )).\n##### (d) Means of sale\nThe Reserve may carry out a sale described in subsections (b) and (c) through\u2014\n**(1)**\nsolicitation and direct contracting with private parties;\n**(2)**\nphysically cleared financial instruments, such as futures contracts through authorized intermediaries;\n**(3)**\noptions contracts directly or through authorized intermediaries; and\n**(4)**\nother transactions, including public auctions, as determined necessary by the Board to support the purposes of the Reserve.\n##### (e) Foreign entities of concern\nThe Reserve may not carry out a sale described in subsections (b) and (c) to a foreign entity of concern.\nIII\nAdministrative provisions\n#### 301.
