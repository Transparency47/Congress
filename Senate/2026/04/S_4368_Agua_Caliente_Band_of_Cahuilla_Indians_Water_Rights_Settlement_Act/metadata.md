25 U.S.C. 403 );\n**(3)**\na due process system for the consideration and determination by the Tribe of any request of an Allottee (or a successor in interest to an Allottee) for an allocation of water for Irrigation on an Allotment, including a process for\u2014\n**(A)**\nappeal and adjudication of any denied or disputed distribution of water;\n**(B)**\nresolution of any contested administrative decision; and\n**(C)**\na requirement that any Allottee asserting a claim relating to the enforcement of rights of the Allottee under the Tribe\u2019s Water Ordinance, including to the quantity of water allocated to land of the Allottee, shall exhaust all remedies available to the Allottee under Tribal Law before initiating an action against the United States or petitioning the Secretary pursuant to subsection (h)(4)(B); and\n**(B)**\neach subsequent amendment to the Tribe\u2019s Water Ordinance that affects the rights of an Allottee is approved by the Secretary.\n**(3) Approval period**\n**(A) In general**\nExcept as provided in subparagraph (B), the Secretary shall approve or disapprove the Tribe\u2019s Water Ordinance amendments described in subsection (i) not later than 180 days after the date on which the amendments are submitted to the Secretary.\n**(B) Extension**\nThe deadline described in subparagraph (A) may be extended by the Secretary after consultation with the Tribe.\n##### (k) Effect\nExcept as otherwise expressly provided in this section, nothing in this Act\u2014\n**(1)**\nauthorizes any action by an Allottee against any individual or entity, or against Agua Caliente, under Federal, State, Tribal, or local law; or\n**(2)**\nalters or affects the status of any action brought pursuant to section 1491(a) of title 28, United States Code.\n##### (l) Domestic water service\nSubject to and in accordance with the Agreement, the Water Districts shall have the authority to provide Domestic Water Service on the Reservation.\n##### (m) Tribe\u2019s surface water rights\n**(1) Tahquitz creek and andreas creek**\nThe Tribe\u2019s water rights in Tahquitz Creek and Andreas Creek, as described in Section VII(A) of the Agreement, shall be held in trust by the United States on behalf of the Tribe and Allottees in accordance with the Agreement and this Act. The Tribe\u2019s Diversion and Use of the surface water shall be subject to and in accordance with the Agreement.\n**(2) Whitewater ranch**\nThe Tribe\u2019s water rights to the surface water at Whitewater Ranch, as described in Section VII(B) of the Agreement, shall be held in trust by the United States on behalf of the Tribe in accordance with the Agreement and this Act. The Tribe\u2019s Diversion and Use of the surface water shall be subject to and in accordance with the Agreement.\n##### (n) Traditional and cultural uses\nThe Tribe and Tribal Members shall have the right to produce and use water from wells, streams, seeps, and springs on the Reservation for traditional and cultural purposes. Such uses shall not be counted as use of the Tribal Water Right as long as it is for Non-consumptive use.\n##### (o) Storage of imported water\nThe Tribe shall have the right to store Imported Water in the Indio Subbasin and recover such stored water, subject to and in accordance with the Agreement.\n#### 6.
