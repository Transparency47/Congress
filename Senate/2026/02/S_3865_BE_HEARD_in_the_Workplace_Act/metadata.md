and\n**(7)**\nlabor organization leaders.\n##### (c) Duties\nThe Task Force shall\u2014\n**(1)**\nidentify strategies and recommend proposals to prevent prohibited harassment in employment; and\n**(2)**\nprovide guidance on effective strategies to prevent prohibited harassment that are specific to industries in which the Task Force determines that harassment is particularly prevalent or severe.\n##### (d) Report\nNot less than once every 5 years, the Commission shall prepare and publish a report on the Commission\u2019s website, which shall be based on the work of the Task Force and shall include\u2014\n**(1)**\na review of the prevalence of prohibited harassment in employment, including the results of the national prevalence survey described in section 111;\n**(2)**\nrecommendations for Federal, State, and local initiatives, reforms, and legislation to prevent prohibited harassment in employment;\n**(3)**\nassessments of the effectiveness of employment policies designed to prevent prohibited harassment in employment by changing behavior and culture;\n**(4)**\nassessments of the effectiveness of processes for investigations into prohibited harassment in employment;\n**(5)**\nassessments of the effectiveness of different types of training to reduce and prevent harassment in employment; and\n**(6)**\nassessments of the effectiveness of other proactive initiatives and interventions to reduce and prevent harassment in employment.\n#### 106. Resource materials on employment climate assessments\n##### (a) In general\nNot later than 1 year after the date of enactment of this Act, the Commission shall develop and make publicly available resource materials for employers on assessing the employment climate, including the occurrence of prohibited harassment in employment, in order to assist such employers in determining the effectiveness of measures the employer takes to prevent and address prohibited harassment in employment.\n##### (b) Employment climate survey\nSuch resource materials shall include a model survey regarding prohibited harassment in employment, which shall be available for an employer to use (at the employer\u2019s discretion and employer\u2019s expense) in order to assess the employment climate. The model survey shall be\u2014\n**(1)**\ndesigned to assess employees\u2019 experiences related to prohibited harassment in employment;\n**(2)**\nfair, unbiased, and scientifically valid to the greatest extent practicable;\n**(3)**\ndesigned to solicit confidential submissions and to provide data without revealing personally identifiable information; and\n**(4)**\ninclusive of individuals required to be afforded protection under section 301.\n##### (c) Contents\nThe model survey may include\u2014\n**(1)**\nquestions designed to assess the prevalence of prohibited harassment in employment;\n**(2)**\nquestions designed to understand whether employees have access to and are familiar with the employer\u2019s nondiscrimination, including anti-harassment, policies and procedures;\n**(3)**\nquestions to assess the employment climate; and\n**(4)**\nany additional questions the Commission determines are consistent with the purposes of this section.\n##### (d) Mandatory employee participation prohibited\nAn employer may not compel or require employees to participate in a survey regarding prohibited discrimination, including harassment, in employment.\n##### (e) Review and revision\nThe Commission shall periodically review and revise the resource materials described in subsection (a) and the model survey developed under subsection (b).\n#### 107. Relationship to other laws\nCompliance with section 101 or 102, or use of materials provided under this subtitle, is not an affirmative defense under applicable employment nondiscrimination laws.\n#### 109.
