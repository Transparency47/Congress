The employee shall be entitled to use the earned paid safe leave and earn additional paid safe leave at the recommencement of employment with the employer.\n##### (d) Uses\nSafe leave earned under this section may be used by an employee for an absence resulting from a qualifying act of violence if the time is for the employee or employee\u2019s family or household member to\u2014\n**(1)**\nseek, receive, or secure counseling;\n**(2)**\nseek or secure temporary or permanent relocation or take steps to secure an existing home;\n**(3)**\nseek, receive, or follow up on assistance from an organization or agency providing services to victims;\n**(4)**\nseek legal assistance or attend legal proceedings, including preparation for or participation in any related administrative, civil, or criminal legal proceeding or other related activities;\n**(5)**\nseek medical attention for physical or psychological injury or disability caused or aggravated by a qualifying act of violence;\n**(6)**\nattend or make arrangements for the funeral or alternative to a funeral or wake of a victim of a qualifying act of violence who died as a result of a qualifying act of violence or grieve the death of a victim who died as a result of a qualifying act of violence;\n**(7)**\nobtain or provide childcare or adult dependent care necessary as a result of a qualifying act of violence;\n**(8)**\nenroll a child in a new school or make a care arrangement;\n**(9)**\naccess financial services or meet with a financial professional to address financial issues resulting from the qualifying act of violence;\n**(10)**\nenroll, renew, or otherwise obtain benefits or public assistance or other services;\n**(11)**\naccess accessibility accommodations, including retrofitting home or vehicle or securing or being fitted for accessibility equipment; or\n**(12)**\ntake any other steps necessary to protect or restore their physical, mental, emotional, and economic well-being or the well-being of a family member recovering from a qualifying act.\n##### (e) Procedures\n**(1) Request**\nSafe leave shall be provided upon the oral or written request of an employee. Such request shall\u2014\n**(A)**\ninclude the expected duration of the period of such leave; and\n**(B)**\nbe provided as soon as practicable after the employee is aware of the need for such period.\n**(2) Certification**\n**(A) In general**\nIf the period in question covers more than 3 workdays, an employer may require that a request for safe leave under this section for a purpose described in subsection (d) be supported\u2014\n**(i)**\nby any form of certification, as determined by the employee, consisting of\u2014\n**(I)**\na sworn statement of the employee or the family or household member, or another person with knowledge of the situation, as the case may be;\n**(II)**\ndocumentation from an employee or volunteer working for a victim services organization, an attorney, a police officer, a medical professional, a social worker, an antiviolence counselor, a member of the clergy, or another professional, affirming that the employee or a family or household member of the employee is a victim of a qualifying act of violence;\n**(III)**\na police or court record indicating that the employee, or a family or household member of the employee, was a victim of a qualifying act of violence;\n**(IV)**\na court order protecting or separating the employee or a family or household member of the employee from the perpetrator of a qualifying act of violence or other evidence from the court or prosecuting attorney that the employee or family or household member has appeared in court or is scheduled to appear in court in a proceeding related to a qualifying act of violence; or\n**(V)**\nother corroborating evidence concerning the employee or family or household member; and\n**(ii)**\nif the victim is the employee\u2019s family or household member, in order to verify the employee\u2019s relationship with the victim, by information that may include a sworn statement of the employee, a birth certificate, a court document, or other corroborating evidence.\n**(B) Survivor information protections**\n**(i) In general**\nThe facts to be disclosed in any certification shall be limited to the minimum necessary to verify a need for the employee to be absent from work in connection with a qualifying act of violence, and the employee shall not be required to explain the details of the qualifying act of violence or how leave will be used.\n**(ii) Limitation on information requirements**\nAn employer may not require an employee, in order to obtain leave under this section, to produce, discuss with the employer, or provide\u2014\n**(I)**\nany additional information, beyond the information enumerated in this subsection that establishes that the employee is eligible for leave under this section; or\n**(II)**\nany information that would compromise the safety of the employee or family or household member in any way.\n**(C) Timeliness**\nThe employee shall provide a copy of such certification to the employer in a timely manner, and not later than 30 days after the first day of the period of leave, to the extent practicable. The employer shall not delay the commencement of the period of leave on the basis that the employer has not yet received the certification.\n**(3) Prohibition**\nAn employer may not require, as a condition of providing safe leave under this title, that the employee involved search for or find a replacement employee to cover the hours during which the employee is using safe leave.\n##### (f) Confidentiality; nondisclosure for victims\n**(1) Confidentiality**\nAll information provided to the employer pursuant to subsection (e), and the fact that the employee or family or household member is a victim of a qualifying act of violence, and the employee has requested or obtained safe leave pursuant to this section, shall be retained in the strictest confidence by the employer, except to the extent that disclosure is\u2014\n**(A)**\nrequested or consented to by the employee in writing; or\n**(B)**\notherwise required by applicable Federal or State law.\n**(2) Confidential communications**\nThe provision of any information under this section does not waive or diminish the confidential or privileged nature of communications between a victim of a qualifying act of violence with one or more of the individuals or entities providing information under subclause (II), (III), (IV), or (V) of clause (i), or clause (ii), of subsection (e)(2)(A).\n**(3) Nondisclosure**\nIf an employer possesses health information about an employee or an employee\u2019s family or household member in connection with a certification under this section, such information shall\u2014\n**(A)**\nbe maintained on a separate form and in a separate file from other personnel information;\n**(B)**\nbe treated as a confidential medical record; and\n**(C)**\nnot be disclosed except to the affected employee or with the written permission of the affected employee.\n##### (g) Employment and benefits\n**(1) Restoration to position**\n**(A) In general**\nExcept as provided in paragraph (2), any employee who takes leave under this section for the intended purpose of the leave shall be entitled, on return from such leave\u2014\n**(i)**\nto be restored by the employer to the position of employment held by the employee when the leave commenced; or\n**(ii)**\nto be restored to an equivalent position with equivalent employment benefits, pay, and other terms and conditions of employment.\n**(B) Loss of benefits**\nThe taking of leave under this section shall not result in the loss of any employment benefit accrued prior to the date on which the leave commenced.\n**(C) Limitations**\nNothing in this subsection shall be construed to entitle any restored employee to any accrual, right, benefit, or position described in section 104(a)(3) of the Family and Medical Leave Act of 1993 ( 29 U.S.C. 2614(a)(3) ).\n**(D) Construction**\nNothing in this paragraph shall be construed to prohibit an employer from requiring an employee on leave under this section to report periodically to the employer on the status and intention of the employee to return to work.\n**(2) Maintenance of health benefits**\nDuring any period that an employee takes leave under this section, the employer shall maintain coverage under any group health plan (meaning a group health plan as defined in section 5000(b)(1) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 or an employee welfare benefit plan as defined in section 3(1) of the Employee Retirement Income Security Act of 1974 ( 29 U.S.C. 1002(1) )) for the duration of such leave at the level and under the conditions coverage would have been provided if the employee had continued in employment continuously for the duration of such leave.\n#### 202. Prohibited acts\n##### (a) Interference with rights\n**(1) In general**\nIt shall be unlawful for any person to interfere with, restrain, deny, or retaliate against an individual because of the exercise of, or the attempt to exercise, any right provided under section 201, including through\u2014\n**(A)**\ndischarging or in any other manner discriminating against (including retaliating against) an individual because the individual has requested, indicated an intent to request, or taken safe leave; or\n**(B)**\nusing the request for or use of such leave as a negative factor in an employment action.\n**(2) Rebuttable presumption of retaliation**\nAny adverse action (including any action described in paragraph (1)) taken against an employee in the 12 month period after an employee takes any leave for which the employee is eligible shall establish a rebuttal presumption that the action of the employer is retaliating against such employee in violation of paragraph (1).\n**(3) Discrimination**\nIt shall be unlawful for any employer to discharge or in any other manner discriminate against any individual for opposing any practice made unlawful by section 201.\n##### (b) Interference with proceedings or inquiries\nIt shall be unlawful for any person to discharge or in any other manner discriminate against any individual because such individual\u2014\n**(1)**\nhas filed any charge, or has instituted or caused to be instituted any proceeding, under or related to section 201;\n**(2)**\nhas given, or is about to give, any information in connection with any inquiry or proceeding relating to any right provided under section 201; or\n**(3)**\nhas testified, or is about to testify, in any inquiry or proceeding relating to any right provided under section 201.\n#### 203. Enforcement\n##### (a) Civil action by affected individuals\n**(1) Liability**\nAny employer that violates section 201 or 202 shall be liable to any individual affected\u2014\n**(A)**\nfor damages equal to\u2014\n**(i)**\nthe greater of $1,000 or the amount of\u2014\n**(I)**\nany wages, salary, employment benefits, or other compensation denied or lost to such individual by reason of the violation; or\n**(II)**\nin a case in which wages, salary, employment benefits, or other compensation has not been denied or lost to the individual, any actual monetary losses sustained by the individual as a direct result of the violation;\n**(ii)**\nthe interest on the amount described in clause (i) calculated at the prevailing rate; and\n**(B)**\nfor such equitable relief as may be appropriate, including employment, reinstatement, and promotion.\n**(2) Right of action**\nAn action to recover the damages or equitable relief prescribed in paragraph (1) may be maintained against any employer in any Federal or State court of competent jurisdiction by any one or more affected individuals for and on behalf of\u2014\n**(A)**\nthe individuals; and\n**(ii)**\nharassment or retaliation described in section 303(a); and\n**(B)**\nused with respect to a public agency and the amount, terms, or conditions of public assistance, includes\u2014\n**(i)**\nfailing to implement, on request from an individual, in response to an actual or threatened qualifying act of violence, a reasonable safety procedure, unless the public agency can demonstrate that granting the request would impose an undue hardship on the operation of the public agency; Prohibited discriminatory acts\n##### (a) Discrimination by employers\nIt shall be an unlawful practice for an employer to fail or refuse to hire or discharge any individual, or otherwise to discriminate (including harassment or retaliation in any form or manner) against any individual with respect to the compensation, terms, conditions, or privileges of employment of the individual, because\u2014\n**(1)**\nthe individual is, or the employer perceives that individual to be, a victim of a qualifying act of violence;\n**(2)**\nthat individual attended, participated in, prepared for, or requested leave related to an event that resulted in the individual becoming a victim of a qualifying act of violence;\n**(3)**\nthat individual, in response to an actual or threatened qualifying act of violence, requested a reasonable accommodation; or\n**(B)**\notherwise required by applicable Federal or State law.\n**(2) Confidential communications**\nThe provision of any information under this section does not waive or diminish the confidential or privileged nature of communications between a victim of a qualifying act of violence with one or more of the individuals or entities providing information as described in section 201(f)(2).\n**(3) Nondisclosure**\nIf an employer possesses health information about an employee (or an employee\u2019s covered family or household member) in connection with a request or determination made under this section, such information shall\u2014\n**(A)**\nbe maintained on a separate form and in a separate file from other personnel information;\n**(B)**\nbe treated as a confidential medical record;
