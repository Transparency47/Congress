United States perfluoroalkyl or polyfluoroalkyl substance policy\n##### (a) General policy\nIt is the policy of the United States that, to the maximum extent practicable and as permitted under applicable law\u2014\n**(1)**\ncontamination of any environmental media by a perfluoroalkyl or polyfluoroalkyl substance should be remediated to levels that do not present an unreasonable risk to public health and the environment;\n**(2)**\nthe destruction and disposal of perfluoroalkyl or polyfluoroalkyl substances\u2014\n**(A)**\nis considered most essential to the elimination of perfluoroalkyl or polyfluoroalkyl substances, which are also known as forever chemicals ; and\n**(B)**\nshould be prioritized as part of any perfluoroalkyl or polyfluoroalkyl substance remediation strategy in a manner that presents the lowest risk of environmental release and the lowest risk to public health and the environment;\n**(3)**\nthe use of perfluoroalkyl or polyfluoroalkyl substances in consumer products should be eliminated; and\n**(4)**\nin cases in which the use of perfluoroalkyl or polyfluoroalkyl substances is essential, in accordance with any applicable report under section 101(h) (including a subsequent report), and no safer alternative for that use is available, those perfluoroalkyl or polyfluoroalkyl substances should be removed or replaced by chemicals, product substitutes, or alternative manufacturing processes that reduce overall risk to human health and the environment, including risks due to chronic, acute, and cumulative impacts.\n##### (b) Federal procurement\n**(1) In general**\nBeginning on the date of enactment of this Act, the heads of Federal agencies, in coordination with the Administrator and the Administrator of General Services, shall, to the maximum extent practicable, eliminate the procurement of products known to contain perfluoroalkyl or polyfluoroalkyl substances.\n**(2) Survey**\nIn carrying out paragraph (1), the heads of Federal agencies may\u2014\n**(A)**\ncarry out surveys of the products procured by the Federal agency to determine whether the products contain perfluoroalkyl or polyfluoroalkyl substances; and\n**(B)**\npause or cease procurement of products that have not been identified as not containing perfluoroalkyl or polyfluoroalkyl substances within a reasonable timeline that accounts for\u2014\n**(i)**\nsurvey completion and product return; and\n**(ii)**\nidentifying and securing safer alternatives for the product.\n##### (c) Best available science\nA determination that an action complies with the policy described in subsection (a) or an action taken under subsection (b) shall be based on the best available science.\n##### (d) Savings provision\nNothing in this section affects any other duty or obligation under Federal law.\n#### 104. Perfluoroalkyl or polyfluoroalkyl substance release phaseout\n##### (a) In general\nBeginning on the date that is 10 years after the date of enactment of this Act, it shall be unlawful for any manufacturer or user to release any quantity of perfluoroalkyl or polyfluoroalkyl substance above the threshold of detection of a detection method for perfluoroalkyl or polyfluoroalkyl substances that is validated by the Administrator in a manner that permits that perfluoroalkyl or polyfluoroalkyl substance to enter the environment.\n##### (b) Rulemaking required\n**(1) In general**\nNot later than 7 years after the date of enactment of this Act and after a period of notice and opportunity for public comment, the Administrator shall finalize a rule that\u2014\n**(A)**\nestablishes a schedule for the phaseout of the releases above the threshold of detection described in subsection (a) by the date described in that subsection; and\n**(B)**\nestablishes applicable detection methods and relevant thresholds.\n**(2) Update**\nThe Administrator may update, in whole or in part, the schedule required under subparagraph (A) of paragraph (1) in accordance with that paragraph.\n**(3) Early adoption**\nThe Administrator may, in accordance with the policy described in section 103(a) and after a period of notice and opportunity for public comment, finalize a rule before the rule required under paragraph (1) that\u2014\n**(A)**\nestablishes a schedule for the phaseout or banning of releases of individual perfluoroalkyl or polyfluoroalkyl substances, mixtures of perfluoroalkyl or polyfluoroalkyl substances, or subclasses of perfluoroalkyl or polyfluoroalkyl substances above the threshold of detection described in subsection (a) by the date described in that subsection; and\n**(B)**\nestablishes applicable detection methods and relevant thresholds.\n##### (c) Savings provision\nNothing in this section affects any other duty or obligation under any other Federal law.\n#### 105.
