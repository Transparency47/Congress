Smart city and community resource guide\n##### (a) Publication\n**(1) In general**\nThe Secretaries, in coordination with the heads of any other applicable Federal agencies, shall create, publish, and maintain, for a period of not less than 6 years beginning on the date that is 1 year after the date of enactment of this Act, a resource guide designed to assist States, communities, and cities in the United States in developing and implementing smart city or community programs.\n**(2) Information included**\nThe Secretaries, in coordination with cities, communities, and industry entities, shall determine which information shall be included in the guide under paragraph (1).\n##### (b) Requirements\nThe guide published under subsection (a)(1)\u2014\n**(1)**\nshall be maintained electronically on a website;\n**(2)**\nshall be provided as an electronic reference guide available to the public free of charge; and\n**(2)**\nto leverage existing complementary efforts.\n##### (d) Outreach\nThe Secretaries, in coordination with the heads of any applicable Federal agencies and in consultation with applicable private sector entities, shall conduct outreach to States, cities, and communities\u2014\n**(1)**\nto provide interested States, cities, and communities with the guide published under subsection (a)(1);\n**(2)**\nto promote the consideration of smart city or community technologies and encourage States and local governments to contribute smart city or community program and activity data to the guide published under subsection (a)(1);\n**(3)**\nto identify\u2014\n**(A)**\nbarriers to smart city or community technology adoption;\n**(B)**\nopportunities to increase the equitable distribution of benefits from the use of smart city or community technology;\n**(C)**\nany necessary research, development, and assistance, including in Tribal, rural, and underserved communities; and\n**(D)**\nlocal government programs that could be included in the guide under subsection (a)(1);\n**(4)**\nto respond to requests for assistance, advice, or consultation from cities and communities; and\n**(5)**\nfor other purposes, as identified by the Secretaries.\nII\nProviding assistance to cities and communities\n#### 201. Technology demonstration grant program\n##### (a) In general\nThe Secretary shall establish a smart city or community regional demonstration grant program under which the Secretary shall conduct demonstration projects focused on advanced smart city or community technologies and systems in a variety of communities, including small- and medium-sized cities and rural and Tribal communities.\n##### (b) Goals\nThe goals of the program established under subsection (a) are\u2014\n**(1)**\nto demonstrate\u2014\n**(A)**\npotential benefits of concentrated investments in smart city or community technologies that are repeatable and scalable across cities of different sizes; and\n**(B)**\nthe efficiency, equity, reliability, and resilience of civic infrastructure and services;\n**(2)**\nto facilitate the adoption of advanced smart city or community technologies and collaboration between small- and medium- to large-sized cities; and\n**(3)**\nto demonstrate protocols and standards that allow for the measurement and validation of the cost savings and performance improvements associated with the installation and use of smart city or community technologies and practices.\n##### (c) Demonstration projects\n**(1) Eligibility**\nSubject to paragraph (2), a unit of local government shall be eligible to receive a grant for a demonstration project under this section.\n**(2) Application; Cooperation**\nTo qualify for a demonstration project under this section, a unit of local government shall\u2014\n**(A)**\nsubmit to the Secretary an application for a grant for a demonstration project at such time and containing such information as the Secretary may require; and\n**(B)**\nagree to follow applicable best practices identified by the Secretaries, in consultation with industry entities and institutions of higher education, to evaluate the effectiveness of the implemented smart city or community technologies to ensure that\u2014\n**(i)**\ntechnologies and interoperability can be assessed;\n**(ii)**\nbest practices can be shared; and\n**(iii)**\ndata can be shared in a public, interoperable, and transparent format.\n**(3) Federal share of cost of technology investments**\nThe Secretary\u2014\n**(A)**\nsubject to subparagraph (B), shall provide to a unit of local government selected under this section for the conduct of a demonstration project a grant in an amount equal to not more than 50 percent of the total cost of technology investments to incorporate and assess qualifying smart city or community technologies in the applicable jurisdiction;
