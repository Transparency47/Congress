Short title\nThis Act may be cited as the Housing not Handcuffs Act of 2025 .\n#### 2. Prohibited activities\n##### (a) Prohibition\nA Federal agency may not impose a penalty on a homeless individual for a permitted use of public land as described under subsection (b).\n##### (b) Permitted use of public lands\nA homeless individual may\u2014\n**(1)**\nconduct life sustaining activities on public land;\n**(2)**\nuse and move freely in places of public accommodation (as such term is defined in section 201 of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 ( 42 U.S.C. 2000a(b) );\n**(3)**\nsolicit, share, accept, or offer food, water, money, or other donations;\n**(4)**\nstore their possessions and enjoy privacy in their personal property to the same degree as property in a private dwelling, which shall not be subject to unreasonable search and seizure;\n**(5)**\npray, meditate, worship, or practice religion;\n**(6)**\noccupy a lawfully parked motor vehicle or a recreational vehicle; and\n**(iii)**\nis available indefinitely to the individual without requiring daily reapplication, at no charge, and must accommodate any disabilities, as well as pets, partners (whether legally married or not), family members, other support persons, and possessions the individual wishes to bring with them;\n**(B)**\nmay be a tiny home or similar structure that includes\u2014\n**(i)**\nlocking doors;\n**(ii)**\nappropriate climate control mechanisms based on the location of the home or structure; and\n**(iii)**\nsanitary and cooking facilities or is a part of a community with common sanitary and cooking facilities; or\n**(C)**\nmay be a permitted parking area that includes sanitary facilities.\n**(2) Jurisdiction**\nAn adequate alternative indoor space in another jurisdiction shall only be deemed an adequate alternative indoor space for the homeless individual if transportation is made available, at no cost to the individual, to ensure the individual can continue to attend to any personal or professional business.\n##### (e) Enforcement\n**(1) Attorney general**\nThe Attorney General may bring a civil action in an appropriate district court of the United States against a government official who violates subsection (a) for equitable relief, including temporary, preliminary, or permanent injunctive relief and costs described under paragraph (3).\n**(2) Private right of action**\nAny individual harmed by an violation of subsection (a) may bring a civil action in an appropriate district court of the United States against a government agency or official that violates subsection (a) for equitable relief, including temporary, preliminary, or permanent injunctive relief and costs described under paragraph (3).\n**(3) Costs**\nWith respect to an action brought under paragraph (1) or (2), the court shall award costs of litigation, as well as reasonable attorney\u2019s fees, to any prevailing plaintiff.
