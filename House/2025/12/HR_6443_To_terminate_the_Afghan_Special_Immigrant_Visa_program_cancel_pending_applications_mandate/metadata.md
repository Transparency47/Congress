Findings\nCongress finds the following:\n**(1)**\nThe Afghan Special Immigrant Visa (SIV) program was created to provide protection to certain individuals who assisted United States missions in Afghanistan.\n**(2)**\nReports by Inspectors General have identified significant deficiencies in vetting, documentation, and identity verification procedures within the SIV process.\n**(3)**\nThe United States has a compelling interest in ensuring that all noncitizens admitted to the United States have undergone rigorous and complete security screening.\n**(4)**\nCongress retains plenary authority over the admission of noncitizens and the structure of creating or terminating immigration categories.\n#### 3. Mandatory review for removability\n##### (a) Individualized determinations required\nFor each Afghan SIV beneficiary, the Secretary shall determine whether the individual\u2014\n**(1)**\nremains eligible for the benefit received;\n**(2)**\nis inadmissible or removable under the Immigration and Nationality Act (INA);\n**(3)**\nengaged in fraud, misrepresentation, or concealment of material facts during the SIV process; and\n**(4)**\nposes a national-security, public-safety, or terrorism-related concern.\n##### (b) Consequences\nIf the Secretary determines that an individual falls within any category listed in subsection (a), the Secretary shall\u2014\n**(1)**\ninitiate rescission of SIV status; and\n**(2)**\ninitiate removal proceedings consistent with due process and the INA.\n##### (c) Priority\nCases involving national-security indicators shall be prioritized for immediate action.\n#### 6.
