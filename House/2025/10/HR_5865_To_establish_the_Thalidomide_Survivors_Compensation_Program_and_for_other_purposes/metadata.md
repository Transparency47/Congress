Short title\nThis Act may be cited as the Thalidomide Survivors Compensation Act of 2025 .\n#### 2. Findings; purpose\n##### (a) Findings\nThe Congress finds the following:\n**(1)**\nThalidomide caused an estimated more than 10,000 birth defects and deaths worldwide during the 1950s and 1960s.\n**(2)**\nThalidomide exposure in utero is known to cause limb deformities and internal organ malformations.\n**(3)**\nAlthough not formally approved in the United States, approximately 5 million thalidomide samples were distributed by over 1,200 physicians through informal clinical trials.\n**(4)**\nThe number of U.S. individuals affected remains undocumented, but research estimates approximately 100 thalidomide survivors are still living in the United States.\n**(5)**\nU.S. thalidomide survivors have faced decades of unreimbursed medical costs, limited accessibility accommodations, and lack of formal diagnosis recognition.\n**(6)**\n46 countries currently provide compensation or medical assistance to thalidomide survivors, including direct payments, pensions, and adaptive living support.\n##### (b) Purpose\nThe purpose of this Act is to allow our thalidomide survivors the ability to live the remainder of their lives with independence and dignity.\n#### 3.
