Reestablishment of species\nThe Secretary, in consultation with the Secretary of Commerce, as appropriate, may carry out activities, based on the best available scientific information, to reestablish covered albatrosses and petrels within the range.\n#### 102. Management of nonnative species\n##### (a) In general\nThe Secretary or the Secretary of Commerce, as appropriate, in consultation with each other and with the heads of other relevant Federal agencies, and consistent with this Act and any other applicable law, may carry out activities to prevent the introduction of, eradicate, or control invasive and nonnative species that have or may have an adverse effect on covered albatrosses and petrels.\n##### (b) Included activities\nThe activities under subsection (a) may include\u2014\n**(1)**\nimplementation of management plans for such invasive or nonnative species;\n**(2)**\nresearch on and development of practical and effective techniques to eradicate or control invasive or nonnative species;\n**(3)**\ndevelopment of regional assessments on established and newly discovered populations of invasive or nonnative species;\n**(4)**\ndevelopment of decision-support tools to prevent the introduction or establishment of invasive or nonnative species;\n**(5)**\ndevelopment of rapid response approaches and techniques;\n**(6)**\ndocumentation of\u2014\n**(A)**\nany invasive or nonnative species that coexist with humans; and\n**(B)**\ndelineation of areas in which eradication or control of those species would be most effective and cost efficient;\n**(7)**\neradication or control of established populations or individuals of invasive or nonnative species; and\n**(8)**\noutreach and education related to\u2014\n**(A)**\nthe impacts of invasive or nonnative species on covered albatrosses and petrels; and\n**(B)**\nthe techniques to eradicate or control those species.\n#### 103. Habitat conservation and restoration\n##### (a) Authority of Secretary\n**(1) Use of other authorities**\nThe Secretary may use authority available under any other laws for the protection of wildlife to conserve, protect, and restore breeding sites of covered albatrosses and petrels, including authority under\u2014\n**(A)**\nthe Migratory Bird Conservation Act ( 16 U.S.C. 715 et seq. );\n**(B)**\nthe Fish and Wildlife Coordination Act ( 16 U.S.C. 661 et seq. );\n**(C)**\nthe Fish and Wildlife Act of 1956 ( 16 U.S.C. 742a , et seq.); and\n**(D)**\nany other cooperative or land-acquisition authority vested in the Secretary.\n**(2) Management plans and conservation measures**\nThe Secretary, in consultation with the Secretary of Commerce, may develop and implement management plans and undertake measures for the conservation and protection of covered albatross and petrel habitat.\n##### (b) Authority of Secretary of Commerce\nThe Secretary of Commerce, in consultation with the Secretary, may\u2014\n**(1)**\ndevelop and implement management plans and undertake conservation measures in marine habitats to ensure the sustainability of living marine resources that provide food for such covered albatrosses and petrels; and\n**(2)**\nuse authority under law to conserve and protect marine habitat important to the conservation of covered albatrosses and petrels, including such authority under\u2014\n**(A)**\nthe National Marine Sanctuaries Act ( 16 U.S.C. 1431 et seq. ); and\n**(B)**\nthe Magnuson-Stevens Act.\n#### 104. Management of human activities\n##### (a) Pollutants and marine debris\n**(1) Research**\nThe Secretary and the Secretary of Commerce may undertake scientific research to assess the effects of pollutants and marine debris on covered albatrosses and petrels.\n**(2) Marine debris**\nThe Secretary and the Secretary of Commerce may each develop and implement conservation measures to minimize the effects of, or threats posed by, marine debris on covered albatrosses and petrels.\n##### (b) Prevention, minimization, or mitigation of take and disturbance\nThe Secretary and the Secretary of Commerce\u2014\n**(1)**\nin consultation with each other and consistent with the Migratory Bird Treaty Act ( 16 U.S.C. 703 et seq. ) and any other authority, may develop and implement measures, including by issuing regulations, to prevent, minimize, or mitigate\u2014\n**(A)**\nthe take of covered albatrosses and petrels\u2014\n**(i)**\non lands or in waters subject to the jurisdiction of the United States; and\n**(ii)**\nto evaluate the effectiveness of any prescribed mitigation measures; and\n**(C)**\nresearch on bycatch-mitigation measures to develop the most practical and effective deterrent measures that reduce such bycatch.\n**(3) Disclosure of information**\n**(A) In general**\nIn carrying out this subsection, the Secretary of Commerce may disclose, as necessary and appropriate, information collected under this Act to the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations, regional fishery-management organizations, or arrangements made pursuant to an international fishery-management agreement, if such organizations or arrangements have policies and procedures to safeguard such information from unintended or unauthorized disclosure.\n**(B) International fishery agreement defined**\nIn this paragraph the term international fishery agreement has the meaning given the term in section 3 of the Magnuson-Stevens Act ( 16 U.S.C. 1802 ).\n#### 105. Education and public awareness\nThe Secretary, and the Secretary of Commerce, in consultation with relevant Regional Fishery Management Councils and others, may\u2014\n**(1)**\nmake information on the conservation status of covered albatrosses and petrels, the threats facing covered albatrosses and petrels, and any actions taken under the Agreement available to\u2014\n**(A)**\nthe scientific, fishing, and seabird conservation communities;\n**(B)**\nthe public;\n**(C)**\nrelevant local authorities;\n**(D)**\nother decisionmakers;\n**(E)**\nother Parties; and\n**(F)**\nother countries;\n**(2)**\ncooperate with other Parties, the Secretariat, and other persons to develop training programs and general information products and exchange resource materials regarding such conservation; and\n**(3)**\nprovide training programs to ensure that personnel responsible for the implementation of this Act have adequate knowledge to implement it effectively.\nII\nProhibited Acts, Permits, and Exemptions\n#### 201. Prohibited acts\n##### (a) In general\nExcept as authorized by a permit, authorization, or exemption under section 202 or 203, it is unlawful\u2014\n**(1)**\nto take any covered albatross or petrel;\n**(2)**\nto commit any act with respect to covered albatrosses or petrels that would be prohibited by section 2 of the Migratory Bird Treaty Act ( 16 U.S.C. 703(a) ) if covered albatrosses or petrels were treated as migratory birds for purposes of that section; or\n**(3)**\nto attempt to engage in any act described in paragraph (1) or (2) of this subsection.\n##### (b) Applicability\n**(1) In general**\nExcept as provided in paragraph (2), the prohibitions under this Act apply\u2014\n**(A)**\non lands and in waters subject to the jurisdiction of the United States; and\n**(B)**\nto vessels and nationals of the United States on lands or in waters beyond the jurisdiction of the United States.\n**(2) Limitation**\nThis section shall not apply with respect to any covered albatross or petrel lawfully taken before the effective date of this Act.\n##### (c) Other prohibited conduct\nIt is unlawful\u2014\n**(1)**\nto violate this Act or any regulation or permit issued under this Act;\n**(2)**\nto refuse permission to board, search, or inspect any vessel that is subject to the control of that person to an officer authorized under section 301(b) to conduct any search, investigation, or inspection to enforce this Act or any regulation or permit issued under this Act;\n**(3)**\nto forcibly assault, resist, oppose, impede, intimidate, or interfere with any officer authorized under section 301(b) in the conduct of any search, investigation, or inspection under this Act;\n**(4)**\nto resist a lawful arrest or detention for any act prohibited by this Act or any regulation or permit issued under this Act; or\n**(5)**\nto interfere with, delay, or prevent, by any means, the apprehension, arrest, or detention of another person, knowing that the other person has committed an act prohibited by this Act or any regulation or permit issued under this Act.\n#### 202. Authorization of take\n##### (a) Permits and regulatory authorization\n**(1) In general**\nThe Secretary, in consultation with the Secretary of Commerce, as appropriate, may, subject to the limitations of the terms of the Agreement and other applicable law, authorize by permit or regulation the deliberate taking of any covered albatrosses and petrels\u2014\n**(A)**\nto enhance the propagation, reestablishment, or survival of those covered albatrosses and petrels;\n**(B)**\non a selective basis and to a limited extent, for scientific, educational, or similar purposes;\n**(C)**\nto accommodate the traditional needs and practices of indigenous people;\n**(D)**\nfor the purposes of assistance or salvage pursuant to the Migratory Bird Treaty Act ( 16 U.S.C. 703 et seq. ); or\n**(E)**\nin other exceptional circumstances as determined by the Secretary.\n**(2) Other take**\nThe Secretary may authorize by permit or regulation the take of covered albatrosses or petrels not otherwise provided for in this Act that is incidental to otherwise lawful activities.\n**(3) Limitations**\nA permit or regulatory authorization under this subsection\u2014\n**(A)**\nshall be limited in area and duration of application; and\n**(B)**\nshall not\u2014\n**(i)**\nresult in the elimination of the favorable conservation status for any covered albatrosses and petrels; or\n**(ii)**\nauthorize any activity otherwise prohibited by any other statute or regulation.\n**(4) Access to breeding sites**\nAny permit or other regulatory authorization under this subsection that authorizes access to breeding sites of covered albatrosses and petrels, including for purposes of scientific research, shall contain terms and conditions to minimize unnecessary disturbance to covered albatrosses and petrels, and to minimize the impact on their habitats.\n##### (b) Exceptions\nThe taking of covered albatrosses and petrels is not prohibited by this Act if\u2014\n**(1)**\nthe taking is necessary to avoid imminent suffering, serious injury, additional injury, or death to any covered albatrosses and petrels hooked or entangled in fishing gear or debris;\n**(2)**\nreasonable care is taken to ensure the safe and expeditious release of the covered albatrosses and petrels; and\n**(3)**\nthe taking is reported to the Secretary of Commerce in a timely manner.\n#### 203. Exemption\n##### (a) Military activities\n**(1) In general**\nIt shall not be a violation of this Act for personnel of the Department of Defense to take covered albatrosses and petrels incidental to military activities.\n**(2) Vessels and aircraft**\nThis Act shall not apply to vessels and aircraft entitled to sovereign immunity under international law.\n**(3) Guidance to avoid or minimize take**\nThe Secretary, in consultation with the Secretary of Commerce and the Secretary of Defense, may issue guidance to minimize, to the extent practicable, the take of covered albatrosses and petrels that is incidental to military activities.\n##### (b) Coast Guard activities\n**(1) Law enforcement**\nNothing in this section shall be considered to limit the authority of the Coast Guard to enforce this or any other Federal law under section 89 of title 14, United States Code.\n**(2) Emergency response**\nIt shall not be a violation of this Act for the Coast Guard to take any covered albatrosses and petrels incidental to any emergency response or search and rescue activity.\n##### (c) Other activities\nTake of any covered albatrosses and petrels is not unlawful if the take was caused by any officer who is authorized by the Secretary, the Secretary of Commerce, or the head of any Federal or State agency that has entered into an agreement with the Secretary or the Secretary of Commerce under section 403, to enforce this Act while performing official duties.\n##### (d) Bycatch of covered albatrosses and petrels in fisheries\nIt shall not be a violation of this Act for any person to take covered albatrosses and petrels as bycatch incidental to otherwise lawful fishing activities, if carried out in accordance with applicable measures to minimize the bycatch of covered albatrosses and petrels undertaken pursuant to section 104(c).\nIII\nPenalties and Enforcement\n#### 301.
