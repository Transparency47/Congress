Organization of ownership investment companies\n##### (a) In general\n**(1) Requirement**\nAn ownership investment company shall be an incorporated body, a limited liability company, or a limited partnership organized and chartered or otherwise existing under State law solely for the purpose of performing the functions and conducting the activities contemplated under this Act, which\u2014\n**(A)**\nif incorporated, has succession for a period of not fewer than 30 years unless sooner dissolved by its shareholders; or\n**(B)**\nif a limited partnership, has succession for a period of not fewer than 10 years, and possesses the powers reasonably necessary to perform such functions and conduct such activities.\n**(2) Area of operation**\nThe area in which an ownership investment company described in paragraph (1) is to conduct its operations, and the establishment of branch offices or agencies (if authorized by the articles), shall be subject to the approval of the Department.\n##### (b) Articles\n**(1) Requirements**\nThe articles of any ownership investment company shall specify\u2014\n**(A)**\nthe objects for which the company is formed in general terms;\n**(B)**\nthe name assumed by the ownership investment company;\n**(C)**\nthe area or areas in which the operations of the ownership investment company are to be carried on;\n**(D)**\nthe place where the principal office of the ownership investment company is to be located; and\n**(E)**\nthe amount and classes of the shares of capital stock of the ownership investment company.\n**(2) Inclusions**\nArticles of an ownership investment company may contain any other provisions not inconsistent with this Act that the ownership investment company may see fit to adopt for the regulation of the business of the ownership investment company and the conduct of the affairs of the ownership investment company.\n**(3) Approval**\nArticles of an ownership investment company and any amendments thereto adopted from time to time shall be subject to the approval of the Secretary.\n##### (c) Issuance of license\n**(1) Submission of application**\n**(A) In general**\nEach applicant to operate as an ownership investment company (including a Prot\u00e9g\u00e9 OIC) under this Act shall submit to the Secretary an application, in a form and including such documentation as may be prescribed by the Secretary.\n**(B) Rolling basis**\nThe Secretary shall accept applications under subparagraph (A) on a rolling basis.\n**(C) Electronic submissions**\nThe Secretary shall allow an applicant under this subsection to electronically submit any document required by this subsection and to provide an electronic signature for any signature that is required on such a document.\n**(2) Procedures**\n**(A) Status**\nNot later than 90 days after the initial receipt by the Secretary of an application under this subsection, the Secretary shall provide the applicant with a written report detailing the status of the application and any requirements remaining for completion of the application.\n**(B) Approval or disapproval**\nWithin 90 days after receiving a completed application submitted in accordance with this subsection and in accordance with such requirements as the Secretary may prescribe by regulation, the Secretary shall\u2014\n**(i)**\napprove the application and issue a license for such operation to the applicant if the requirements of this section are satisfied; or\n**(ii)**\ndisapprove the application and notify the applicant in writing of the disapproval.\n**(3) Matters considered**\n**(A) In general**\nIn reviewing and processing any application under this subsection, the Secretary\u2014\n**(i)**\nshall determine whether\u2014\n**(I)**\nthe applicant meets the requirements of subsections (a) and (b) of section 6; and\n**(II)**\nthe management of the applicant is qualified and has the knowledge, experience, and capability necessary to comply with this Act;\n**(ii)**\nshall take into consideration\u2014\n**(I)**\nthe need for and availability of financing for a covered business concern in the geographic area in which the applicant is to commence business;\n**(II)**\nthe general business reputation of the owners and management of the applicant; and\n**(III)**\nthe probability of successful operations of the applicant, including adequate profitability and financial soundness; and\n**(iii)**\nshall not take into consideration any projected shortage or unavailability of leverage.\n**(B) Additional matters considered for ownership investment companies**\n**(i) Investment track record**\nExcept as provided in clause (ii), an applicant for a license to operate as an ownership investment company shall submit to the Secretary proof that the managers of the applicant have a track record of managing investments, including structured investments, realized or unrealized, in an employee stock ownership plan or eligible worker-owned cooperative.\n**(ii) Advisory requirement**\nAn applicant that does not have an investment track record described in clause (i) or that is a Prot\u00e9g\u00e9 OIC shall submit to the Secretary evidence that the applicant has retained or will retain a legal, accounting, or financial advisory firm with not fewer than 5 years of experience in structuring employee stock ownership plans or eligible worker-owned cooperatives.\n**(iii) Limitation**\nThe Secretary may not reject an applicant for a license to operate as an ownership investment company solely because the applicant lacks a sufficient track record in realized investments if the applicant demonstrates an otherwise successful investment track record that includes unrealized covered investments.\n**(C) Provisional approval**\n**(i) In general**\nThe Secretary may provide provisional approval for a license to participate in the facility as an ownership investment company (including a Prot\u00e9g\u00e9 OIC) for a period not to exceed 1 year to an investment firm submitting an application under this subsection or\u2014\n**(I)**\nthat does not meet the minimum private capital requirements under section 6(a) necessary for licensing under this subsection at the time of application;\n**(II)**\nthat states an intent to more effectively raise capital commitments in private markets with a license; and\n**(III)**\nthat states an intent to more precisely request the desired amount of leverage contingent on securing capital from private market investors.\n**(ii) Capital requirements**\nAn applicant granted provisional approval under clause (i) shall not be eligible to receive leverage until the applicant satisfies the requirements of section 6(a).\n**(D) Fees**\n**(i) In general**\nThe Secretary may prescribe fees to be paid by each applicant for a license to operate as an ownership investment company (including a Prot\u00e9g\u00e9 OIC) under this Act.\n**(ii) Use of amounts**\nFees collected under this subparagraph\u2014\n**(I)**\nshall be deposited in the account for salaries and expenses of the Department; and\n**(II)**\nare authorized to be appropriated solely to cover the costs of licensing examinations.\n##### (d) 1940 and 1980 Act Companies\n**(1) In general**\n**(A) Application**\nA 1940 Act Company or 1980 Act Company is eligible to apply for a license under this Act.\n**(B) Eligibility of licensees**\nA licensee that is not registered as a 1940 Act Company or 1980 Act Company is eligible to apply for approval from the Secretary to convert to a 1940 Act Company or 1980 Act Company.\n**(2) Regulation**\nA 1940 Act Company or 1980 Act Company that is a licensee may elect to be taxed as a regulated investment company for purposes of section 851 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 ( 26 U.S.C. 851 ), provided that the licensee making such election may make distributions only as permitted under the applicable guidance or regulations that the Secretary may prescribe.\n#### 5. Prot\u00e9g\u00e9 OIC Program\n##### (a) Establishment\nThe Secretary shall establish a program to be known as the Prot\u00e9g\u00e9 OIC Program under which a manager of an ownership investment company that is not a Prot\u00e9g\u00e9 OIC may enter into a written agreement approved by the Secretary to provide guidance and assistance to a Prot\u00e9g\u00e9 OIC with respect to\u2014\n**(1)**\napplying for a license for the Prot\u00e9g\u00e9 OIC to operate as an ownership investment company; and\n**(2)**\nmanagement of the ownership investment company after licensure.\n##### (b) Application\nAfter entering into a written agreement described in subsection (a), the Prot\u00e9g\u00e9 OIC shall apply for a license under section 4(c).\n##### (c) Selection\nThe Secretary may grant a license to a Prot\u00e9g\u00e9 OIC to operate as an ownership investment company under section 4(c) based on the investment track record of 1 or more of the managers that have entered into a written agreement described in subsection (a) of this section with the applicant Prot\u00e9g\u00e9 OIC.\n##### (d) Requirements for managers\nIf a manager enters into a written agreement described in subsection (a)\u2014\n**(1)**\nthe manager may hold a minority financial interest in the ownership investment company that is to be managed by the Prot\u00e9g\u00e9 OIC;\n**(2)**\nthe otherwise applicable maximum amount of outstanding leverage that may be made available to any 1 licensed company of the manager under section 7(b)(6)(B)(i)(I) shall be increased by $17,500,000; and\n**(3)**\nthe otherwise applicable maximum amount of outstanding leverage that may be made available to any 2 or more licensed companies that are commonly controlled by the manager under section 7(b)(6)(B)(i)(II) shall be increased by $35,000,000.\n#### 6. Capital requirements\n##### (a) Amount\n**(1) In general**\nThe private capital of each licensee shall be not less than $10,000,000.\n**(2) Adequacy**\nIn addition to the requirement under paragraph (1), the Secretary shall\u2014\n**(A)**\ndetermine whether the private capital of each licensee is adequate to assure a reasonable prospect that the licensee will be operated soundly and profitably, and managed actively and prudently in accordance with the articles of the licensee; and\n**(B)**\ndetermine whether the licensee will be able, both prior to licensing and prior to approving any request for financing, to make periodic payments on any debt of the licensee that is interest bearing and shall take into consideration the income that the licensee anticipates on the contemplated investments of the licensee, the experience of the owners and managers of the licensee, the history of the licensee as an entity, if any, and the financial resources of the licensee.\n##### (b) Diversification of ownership\nThe Secretary shall ensure that the management of each licensee (including Prot\u00e9g\u00e9 OICs) is sufficiently diversified from and unaffiliated with the ownership of the licensee in a manner that ensures independence and objectivity in the financial management and oversight of the investments and operations of the licensee.\n#### 7. Borrowing power\n##### (a) In general\nEach ownership investment company shall have authority to borrow money and to issue its securities, promissory notes, or other obligations under such general conditions and subject to such limitations and regulations as the Secretary may prescribe.\n##### (b) Authority To guarantee\n**(1) Grant of authority**\nTo encourage the formation and growth of ownership investment companies the Secretary may, when authorized in appropriation Acts, guarantee the timely payment of all principal and interest as scheduled on debentures issued by ownership investment companies.\n**(2) Terms and conditions**\nGuarantees made under paragraph (1) may be made by the Secretary on such terms and conditions as the Secretary deems appropriate, pursuant to regulations issued by the Secretary.\n**(3) Full faith and credit**\nThe full faith and credit of the United States is pledged to the payment of all amounts that may be required to be paid under any guarantee under this subsection.\n**(4) Status of debentures**\nDebentures guaranteed by the Department under this subsection shall be subordinate to any other debenture bonds, promissory notes, or other debts and obligations of an ownership investment company, unless the Secretary, in the exercise of reasonable investment prudence and in considering the financial soundness of such ownership investment company, determines otherwise.\n**(5) Term and interest rate**\nA debenture issued under this subsection may be issued for a term of not to exceed 15 years and shall bear interest at a rate not less than a rate determined by the Secretary of the Treasury, taking into consideration the current average market yield on outstanding marketable obligations of the United States with remaining periods to maturity comparable to the average maturities on such debentures, adjusted to the nearest 1/8 of 1 per centum, plus an additional charge, in an amount established annually by the Secretary, as necessary to reduce to 0 the cost (as defined in section 502 of the Federal Credit Reform Act of 1990 ( 2 U.S.C. 661a )) to the Department guaranteeing debentures under this Act, which amount may not exceed 1.38 percent per year, and which shall be paid to and retained by the Department.\n**(6) Additional restrictions and limitations**\nA debenture issued under this subsection\u2014\n**(A)**\nshall include such other terms as the Department may fix; and\n**(B)**\nshall be subject to the following restrictions and limitations:\n**(i)**\n**(I)**\nThe maximum amount of outstanding leverage made available to any 1 ownership investment company licensed under section 4(c) that is not a Prot\u00e9g\u00e9 OIC may not exceed the lesser of\u2014\n**(aa)**\n100 percent licenses under common control.\n**(iii)**\nThe maximum amount of outstanding leverage made available under the facility established under section 3 to any 1 Prot\u00e9g\u00e9 OIC may not to exceed the lesser of\u2014\n**(I)**\n100 percent of the private capital of the Prot\u00e9g\u00e9 OIC; or\n**(II)**\n$100,000,000.\n**(iv)**\n**(I)**\nIn calculating the outstanding leverage of a company for the purposes of subclauses (I) and (II) of clause (i), the Secretary shall not include the amount of the cost basis of any covered investment made by the ownership investment company in a covered business concern that\u2014\n**(aa)**\nconducts in the United States research and development, engineering, or production activities necessary or incidental to manufacturing;\n**(bb)**\noperates in a critical industry or critical technology area identified by the Secretary to be vital to maintaining the national or economic security of the United States; and\n**(cc)**\nis headquartered in the United States, or will be headquartered in the United States immediately following the transaction in the case of a covered investment described in section 2(6)(A).\n**(II)**\nThe exclusion of amounts in subclause (I) shall not exceed a total of $75,000,000 or 25 percent of private capital of such company, whichever is less.\n**(III)**\nSubclause (I) shall not apply to any Prot\u00e9g\u00e9 OIC.\n**(v)**\nNothing in this paragraph shall prevent licensees with 1 or more small business investment companies licensed under section 301(c) of the Small Business Investment Act of 1958 ( 15 Calculation of subsidy rate\nAll fees and interest received and retained by the Department under this section shall be included in the calculations made by the Director of the Office of Management and any written statement required under this Act, or under any regulation issued under this Act by the Secretary;\n**(2)**\nif any written statement required under this Act, or under any regulation issued under this Act by the Secretary, fails to state a material fact necessary in order to make the statement not misleading in the light of the circumstances under which the statement was made;\n**(3)**\nfor willful or repeated violation, or willful or repeated failure to observe, any provision of this Act;\n**(4)**\nfor willful or repeated violation of, or willful or repeated failure section.\n##### (b) Order To cease and desist\n**(1) Issuance**\nWhere a licensee or any other person has not complied with any provision of this Act, or of any regulation issued under this Act by the whom an order has been issued until such licensee complies with an order issued under this subsection.\n##### (c) Orders To show cause\n**(1) Issuance**\nBefore revoking or suspending a license pursuant to subsection (a) or issuing a cease and desist order pursuant to subsection (b), the Secretary shall serve upon the licensee and any other person involved an order to show cause why an order revoking or suspending the license or a cease and desist order should not be issued.\n**(2) Contents**\nAny order to show cause issued under paragraph (1) shall\u2014\n**(A)**\ncontain a statement of the matters of fact and law asserted by the Department and the legal authority and jurisdiction under which a hearing is to be held; and\n**(B)**\nset forth that a hearing will be held before the
