Short title\nThis Act may be cited as the No Gratuitous Overcharging for Ubiquitous Global Exports Act or the No GOUGE Act .\n#### 2. the United States a tariffed good at an unreasonably high price during the 5-year period that follows the date on which any tariff or planned tariff applicable to such tariffed good entered into force or was demonstrated by a written or spoken statement, as the case may be.\n**(2) Unreasonably high price**\n**(A) In general**\nFor purposes of this section, a person is selling or offering for sale a tariffed good at an unreasonably high price if such person\u2014\n**(i)**\nhas raised the price of a tariffed good that is a final good by more than the amount of the costs directly repurchase programs) incurred by such person in providing such good that demonstrate a tariff was not used by such person as a pretext for a price increase; or\n**(iii)**\nhas raised the price of a tariffed good that is a component by more than the amount of the costs directly generated\u2014\n**(I)**\nby the imposition of a tariff with respect to such component; or\n**(II)**\nby\u2014\n**(aa)**\nthe imposition of a tariff with respect to such component; and\n**(bb)**\nadditional costs (not including costs relating to increased executive compensation or share repurchase programs) incurred by such person in providing such good that demonstrate a tariff was not used by such person as a pretext for a price increase.\n**(B) Planned tariffs**\nWith respect to a tariffed good described in section 2(7)(D), no costs may be determined to be directly generated by the imposition of a tariff with respect to such good before the date on which a tariff enters into force with respect to such good.\n**(C) Baseline price determinations**\nFor purposes of this paragraph, the price of a tariffed good prior to the date on which any tariff or planned tariff applicable the first year beginning after the date of the enactment of this Act, and annually thereafter, the Commission shall adjust the amount specified in paragraph (1) by the percentage change in the consumer price index for all urban consumers published by the Bureau of Labor Statistics with respect to the 12-month period preceding the date of such adjustment.\n##### (c) Presumption of leverage**\n**(A) Characteristics**\nFor purposes of this subsection, a person has unfair leverage if such person or the ultimate parent entity of such person\u2014\n**(i)**\nearned at least Labor Statistics with respect to the 12-month period preceding the date of such adjustment.\n**(C) Considerations**\nIn promulgating regulations under subparagraph (A)(ii), the Commission shall is identical or substantially similar to a tariffed good.\n##### (d) Regulations\n**(1) In general**\nThe Commission may promulgate, in accordance with section 553 of title 5, United States Code, such regulations as may be necessary to carry out this section.\n**(2) Interagency consultation**\nThe Commission, in promulgating regulations under this subsection, shall consult with the United States deceptive acts or practices**\nA violation of this section or a regulation promulgated under this section shall be treated as a violation of a regulation under section 18(a)(1)(B) of the Federal Trade
