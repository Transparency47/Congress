## Summary

**Alpha-gal Allergen Inclusion Act**

This bill expands the definition of *major food allergen* to include galactose-alpha-1,3-galactose (commonly known as *alpha-gal* ). Under current law, food labels generally must identify each major food allergen found in labeled food products.

(Certain tick bites cause an allergic condition known as alpha-gal syndrome that can result in an allergy to the alpha-gal molecule, which is found in red meat and other products made from mammals.)

A BILL
To amend the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act to add alpha-gal to the definition of major food allergen .
#### 1. Short title
This Act may be cited as the Alpha-gal Allergen Inclusion Act .
#### 2. Adding alpha-gal to definition of major food allergen
##### (a) In general
Section 201(qq) of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act ( 21 U.S.C. 321(qq) ) is amended by adding at the end the following:
(3) (A) Galactose-alpha-1,3-galactose (commonly known as alpha-gal ). (B) For purposes of this subparagraph, the term galactose-alpha-1,3-galactose includes— (i) ingredients derived from non-catarrhine primate mammals; and (ii) red algae in the order Gigartinales. (C) For purposes of this subparagraph, the term galactose-alpha-1,3-galactose does not include mammal-derived ingredients for which the Secretary determines there is no galactose-alpha-1,3-galactose above an established, detectable limit (such as ingredients made from alpha-gal-knockout mammals). .
##### (b) Effective date
The amendment made by subsection (a) shall apply beginning on the date that is 18 months after the date of enactment of this Act.
