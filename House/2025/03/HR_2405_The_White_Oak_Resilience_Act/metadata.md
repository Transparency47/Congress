Department of the Interior white oak review and restoration\n##### (a) Assessment\n**(1) In general**\nThe Secretary of the Interior shall carry out an assessment of land under the administrative jurisdiction of the Department of the Interior, including fish and wildlife refuges and abandoned mine land, to evaluate\u2014\n**(A)**\nwhether white oak is present on such land; and\n**(B)**\nthe potential to restore white oak forests on such land.\n**(2) Use of information**\nIn carrying out the assessment under paragraph (1), the Secretary may use information from sources other than the Department of the Interior, including from the White Oak Initiative and the Forest Service.\n**(3) Report**\nNot later than 90 days after the date of enactment of this section, the Secretary shall submit to Congress, and make publicly available on the website of the Department of the Interior, a report regarding the results of the assessment carried out under this subsection.\n##### (b) Pilot projects\nAfter the date on which the report required under subsection (a)(3) is submitted, the Secretary shall establish and carry out 5 pilot projects in different areas of land described in subsection (a)(1) to restore and naturally regenerate white oak.\n##### (c) Authority To enter into cooperative agreements\nThe Secretary may enter into cooperative agreements to carry out the pilot projects required under subsection (b).\n##### (d) Sunset\nThe authority under this section shall terminate on the date that is 7 years after the date of enactment of this Act.\n#### 6. White oak regeneration and upland oak habitat\n##### (a) Establishment\nNot later than 180 days after the date of enactment of this Act, the Secretary of Agriculture shall establish a non-regulatory program to be known as the White Oak and Upland Oak Habitat Regeneration Program (in this section referred to as the Program ).\n##### (b) Duties\nIn carrying out the Program, the Secretary shall\u2014\n**(1)**\ndraw upon the best available science and management plans for species of white oak to identify, prioritize, and implement restoration and conservation activities that will improve the growth of white oak within the United States;\n**(2)**\ncollaborate and coordinate with the White Oak Restoration Initiative Coalition to prioritize white oak restoration initiatives;\n**(3)**\nadopt a white oak restoration strategy that\u2014\n**(A)**\nsupports the implementation of a shared set of science-based restoration and conservation activities developed in accordance with paragraph (1);\n**(B)**\ntargets cost effective projects with measurable results;
