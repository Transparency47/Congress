(3) Transnational repression The term transnational repression means an action of a foreign government or an agent of a foreign government that satisfies each of the following: (A) The action involves\u2014 (i) any effort intended to coerce, harass, or digitally or physically threaten, including by force or reasonable fear of death or serious bodily injury or imprisonment of a person or an immediate family member of a person, a person to take an action in the interest of such a foreign government; (ii) any effort intended to harass or coerce, including by force or fear, a person to forebear from exercising their First Amendment rights or any other right guaranteed to the person by the Constitution or laws of the United States, or to retaliate against a person for having exercised such a right; (iii) an extrajudicial killing; or (iv) any act intended to further the efforts specified in clause (i), (ii), or (iii).
