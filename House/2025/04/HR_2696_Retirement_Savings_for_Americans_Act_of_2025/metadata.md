Amounts in the account of a former participant shall remain in the Fund until distributed in accordance with subsection (b).\n##### (b) Former participant withdrawal options\nSubject to section 109, a former participant is entitled to and may elect to withdraw from the Fund the balance of the former participant\u2019s account as\u2014\n**(1)**\nan annuity;\n**(2)**\na single payment;\n**(3)**\n2 or more substantially equal payments to be made not less frequently than annually; or\n**(4)**\nany combination of payments described in paragraphs (1) through (3) as the Executive Director may prescribe by regulation.\n##### (c) Additional former participant withdrawal options\n**(1) In general**\nIn addition to the right provided under subsection (b) to withdraw the balance of the account, a former participant may make 1 or more withdrawals of any amount in the same manner as a single payment is made in accordance with subsection (b)(2) from the former participant\u2019s account.\n**(2) Transfers to retirement plans**\n**(A) In general**\nA former participant may request that the amount withdrawn from the Fund under paragraph (1) be transferred to an existing retirement plan.\n**(B) Transfers**\nThe Executive Director shall make each transfer directly to an existing retirement plan identified by the former participant for whom the transfer is made. A transfer shall not be made under the preceding sentence until the Executive Director receives from the former participant the information required by the Executive Director specifically to identify the existing retirement plan to which the transfer is to be made.\n**(3) Limitations**\nWithdrawals under this subsection shall be subject to such other limitations or conditions as the Executive Director may prescribe by regulation.\n##### (d) Payment of annuities\nThe Board shall prescribe methods of payment of annuities under this Act substantially similar to those provided for under section 8434 of title 5, United States Code.\n##### (e) Former participant changes to elections\n**(1) In general**\nSubject to section 109, a former participant may change an election previously made under this section, except that in the case of an election to receive an annuity, a former participant may not change an election under this section on or after the date on which an annuity contract is purchased to provide for the annuity elected by the former participant.\n**(2) Distributions made**\nA former participant may not return a distribution once made pursuant to an election under this section.\n##### (f) Survivor rights\n**(1) In general**\nIf a participant or a former participant dies without having made an election under subsection (b) or after having elected an annuity under subsection (b) but before making an election for payments to a survivor rights under section 8434 of title 5, United States Code, an amount equal to the value of that individual\u2019s account (as of death) shall, subject to any decree, order, or agreement referred to in section 109, be paid in a manner consistent with the requirements of section 109.\n**(2) Maintenance of account**\nNotwithstanding section 109, if a participant or former participant dies and has designated as sole or partial beneficiary the spouse of the participant or former participant at the time of death, or, if a participant or former participant dies with no designated beneficiary and is survived by a spouse, the spouse may maintain the portion of the participant or former participant\u2019s account to which the spouse is entitled in accordance with the following terms:\n**(A)**\nSubject to the limitations of subparagraph (B), the spouse shall have the same withdrawal options under subsection (b) as a former participant.\n**(B)**\nThe spouse may not make withdrawals under subsection (h) or (i).\n**(C)**\nThe spouse may not make contributions or transfers to the account.\n**(D)**\nThe account shall be disbursed upon the death of the surviving spouse of the participant or former participant and shall not be maintained by a beneficiary or surviving spouse of the surviving spouse who inherited the account.\n**(3) Regulations**\nThe Executive Director shall prescribe regulations to carry out this subsection.\n##### (g) Small Balance Accounts\nNotwithstanding subsection (b), if a former participant\u2019s account balance is less than an amount that the Executive Director prescribes by regulation, the Executive Director shall pay the nonforfeitable account balance to the participant in a single payment. The Executive Director may prescribe more than 1 balance amount for payment under this subsection based on age of the former participant.\n##### (h) Loans\n**(1) In general**\nA participant or former participant may apply to the Board for permission to borrow from the participant or former participant\u2019s account an amount not exceeding the value of that portion of such account which is attributable to contributions made by the participant or former participant. Before a loan is issued, the Executive Director shall provide to the participant or former participant in writing with appropriate information concerning the cost of the loan relative to other sources of financing, as well as the lifetime cost of the loan, including the difference in interest rates between the funds offered by the Fund and any other effect of such loan on the participant or former participant\u2019s final account balance.\n**(2) Special rules**\n**(A) In general**\nLoans under this subsection shall be available to all participants and former participants on a reasonably equivalent basis, and shall be subject to such other conditions as the Board may prescribe by regulation, which shall be as equivalent as practically possible to those provided for under the Thrift Savings Plan. The restrictions of section 206(c)(1) shall not apply to loans made under this subsection.\n**(B) Limitation based on tax treatment**\nA loan may not be made under this subsection to the extent that the loan would be treated as a taxable distribution under section 72(p) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986.\n**(C) Spousal protections**\nA loan may not be made under this subsection unless the requirements of section 109 are satisfied.\n##### (i) Voluntary distributions\n**(1) In general**\nA participant may apply, before becoming a former participant, to the Board for permission to withdraw an amount from the participant\u2019s account based upon\u2014\n**(A)**\nthe participant having attained age 59 1/2 ; or\n**(B)**\nfinancial hardship.\n**(2) Limitations**\nA withdrawal under paragraph (1)(B) shall be available only for an amount not exceeding the value of that portion of such account which is attributable to contributions made by the participant. Withdrawals under paragraph (1) shall be subject to such other limitations or conditions as the Executive Director may prescribe by regulation, which shall be as equivalent as practically possible to those provided for under the Thrift Savings Plan.\n**(3) Spousal protections**\nA withdrawal may not be made under this subsection unless the requirements of section 109 are satisfied.\n##### (j) Involuntary distributions\n**(1) In general**\nA participant shall receive a distribution from the Fund if the participant\u2019s gross income for a taxable year exceeds the dollar threshold (as adjusted by the Secretary of the Treasury) established under section 414(q)(1)(B) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986.\n**(2) Amount of distribution**\nThe amount of a distribution under paragraph (1) shall be equal to the sum of such participant\u2019s contributions to the Fund for the taxable year for which such distribution is required under paragraph (1), increased by any gains attributable to such contributions, and decreased by any losses attributable to such contributions, any early withdrawal penalties, and any expenses associated with making such distribution.\n**(3) Process for distribution**\n**(A) Notice to participant**\nThe Executive Director shall provide notice to a participant subject to a distribution under paragraph (1) not later than 7 days after the Executive Director determines that such participant is subject to such distribution, based on information regarding participants' gross income provided by the Secretary of the Treasury.\n**(B) Method of distribution**\nNot later than 30 days after receiving notice under subparagraph (A), a participant may elect to direct that a distribution under paragraph (1) be made\u2014\n**(i)**\nin the case of an eligible rollover distribution (as defined in section 402(c) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986), to an eligible retirement plan (as defined in such section of such code); or\n**(ii)**\ndirectly to such participant.\n**(C) Default election**\nIn the case of a participant who fails to make an election within the period described in subparagraph (B), the Executive Director shall make the distribution directly to such participant.\n**(4) Tax treatment of involuntary distribution**\nA distribution made under paragraph (1) directly to the participant under subparagraph (B)(ii) or (C) shall be treated as an early distribution from a qualified retirement plan pursuant to section 72(t) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 to the extent such distribution does not consist of participant contributions to the Fund.\n##### (k) Treatment as Roth distributions\nThe rules of sections 408(d) and 408A(d) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 shall apply to distributions from the Fund in the same manner as if such Fund were a Roth IRA. For purposes of the preceding sentence, contributions made under section 25F of such Code shall be treated as employer contributions which were not includible in gross income.\n#### 107. Accounts\n##### (a) In general\nThe Executive Director shall establish and maintain an account for each participant who makes contributions under section 105(a), or for whom contributions are made under section 25F of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, to the Fund.\n##### (b) Account balances\nThe balance in a participant\u2019s account is the excess of\u2014\n**(1)**\nthe sum of\u2014\n**(A)**\nall contributions made to the Fund by the participant under section 105(a);\n**(B)**\nall contributions made to the Fund for the benefit of the participant by the Secretary of the Treasury under section 25F of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986; and\n**(C)**\nthe total amount of the allocations made to and reduction made in the account pursuant to subsection (c); over\n**(2)**\nthe amounts paid out of the Fund with respect to such participant under this title.\n##### (c) Allocation of earnings and losses\nPursuant to regulation prescribed by the Executive Director, the Executive Director shall allocate to each account an amount equal to a pro rata share of the net earnings and net losses from each investment of sums in the Fund attributed to sums credited to such account, reduced by the appropriate share of the administrative expenses paid out of the net earnings under section 101(e) as determined by the Executive Director.\n#### 108. Tax treatment\nExcept as otherwise provided in this Act, for purposes of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, rules similar to the rules that apply with respect to the Thrift Savings Fund (including the rules of section 8440 of title 5, United States Code) shall apply with respect to the American Worker Retirement Fund.\n#### 109. Spousal protections; survivor rights\nThe provisions for spousal protections and court orders under section 8435 and 8467 of title 5, United States Code, respectively, shall apply in the same manner to governance of the Fund and to accounts of participants and former participants as such sections are applied with respect to the Thrift Savings Plan and its accounts. The Executive Director shall issue regulations that establish spousal protections and survivor rights with respect to participants and former participants that are as equivalent as practically possible to those provided for under the Thrift Savings Plan pursuant to chapter 84 of title 5, United States Code.\nII\nThe American Worker Retirement Plan Investment Management System\n#### 201. The American Worker Retirement Investment Board\n##### (a) Establishment\nThere is established in the executive branch of the Government the American Worker Retirement Investment Board.\n##### (b) Composition\nThe Board shall be composed of\u2014\n**(1)**\n3 members appointed by the President, of whom 1 shall be designated by the President as Chair;\n**(2)**\n1 member appointed by the President after taking into consideration the recommendation made by the majority leader of the Senate in consultation with the minority leader of the Senate; and\n**(3)**\n1 member appointed by the President after taking into consideration the recommendation made by the Speaker of the House of Representatives in consultation with the minority leader of the House of Representatives.\n##### (c) Senate confirmation\nAppointments under subsection (b) shall be made with the advice and consent of the Senate.\n##### (d) Qualifications\n**(1) In general**\nMembers of the Board shall have substantial experience, training, and expertise in the management of financial investments and pension benefit plans.\n**(2) Disqualification**\nNo member of the Board may be an officer or employee of the Federal Government.\n##### (e) Terms; vacancies\n**(1) Terms**\nA member of the Board shall be appointed for a term of 4 years, except that of the members first appointed under subsection (b)\u2014\n**(A)**\nthe Chair shall be appointed for a term of 4 years;\n**(B)**\nthe members appointed under paragraphs (2) and (3) of subsection (b) shall be appointed for terms of 3 years; and\n**(C)**\nthe remaining members shall be appointed for terms of 2 years.\n**(2) Vacancies**\n**(A) In general**\nA vacancy on the Board shall be filled in the manner in which the original appointment was made and shall be subject to any conditions which applied with respect to the original appointment.\n**(B) Term**\nAn individual chosen to fill a vacancy shall be appointed for the unexpired term of the member replaced.\n**(C) Expiration**\nThe term of any member shall not expire before the date on which the member\u2019s successor takes office.\n##### (f) Board duties\nThe Board shall\u2014\n**(1)**\nestablish policies for\u2014\n**(A)**\nthe investment and management of the Fund; and\n**(B)**\nthe administration of title I of this Act;\n**(2)**\nhire and set the compensation for the Executive Director;\n**(3)**\nreview the performance of investments made for the Fund;\n**(4)**\nreview and approve the budget of the Board; and\n**(5)**\ndevelop evidence-based financial literacy requirements for participants in the Fund, including requirements for financial literacy interventions to occur prior to a participant\u2014\n**(A)**\ntaking an early withdrawal from their account at the Fund pursuant to section 106(i); and\n**(B)**\ntaking a loan from such account pursuant to section 106(h).\n##### (g) Board authorities; investment limitations\n**(1) In general**\nThe Board may\u2014\n**(A)**\nadopt, alter, and use a seal;\n**(B)**\nexcept as provided in paragraph (2), direct the Executive Director to take such action as the Board considers appropriate to carry out the provisions of this Act and the policies of the Board;\n**(C)**\nupon the concurring votes of 4 members, remove the Executive Director from office for good cause shown; and\n**(D)**\ntake such other action as may be necessary to carry out the functions of the Board.\n**(2) Exception**\nExcept in the case of investments under section 102(b)(2), the Board may not direct the Executive Director to invest or to cause to be invested any sums in the Fund in a specific asset or to dispose of or cause to be disposed of any specific asset of such Fund.\n##### (h) Board responsibilities\nThe members of the Board shall discharge their responsibilities under this Act solely in the interest of participants and beneficiaries.\n##### (i) Budget\nThe Board shall prepare and submit to the President, and, at the same time, to the appropriate committees of Congress, an annual budget of the expenses and other items relating to the Board which shall be included as a separate item in the budget required to be transmitted to the Congress under section 1105 of title 31, United States Code.\n##### (j) Legislative recommendations\nThe Board may submit to the President, and, at the same time, shall submit to each House of the Congress, any legislative recommendations of the Board relating to any of its functions under this title.\n#### 202. The American Worker Retirement Plan Advisory Council\n##### (a) Establishment\nThe Board shall establish an American Worker Retirement Plan Advisory Council. The Council shall be composed of 7 members appointed by the Chair of the Board in accordance with subsection (b).\n##### (b) Appointment\nThe Chair shall appoint 7 members of the Council, of whom\u2014\n**(1)**\n3 shall be appointed who have experience managing investment funds;\n**(2)**\n2 shall be appointed who have experience operating small businesses; and\n**(3)**\n2 shall be appointed who have experience providing investment advice to small businesses and low-income workers.\n##### (c) Head of council; terms; vacancies\n**(1) In general**\nThe Chair of the Board shall designate 1 member of the Council to serve as head of the Council.\n**(2) Term**\nA member of the Council shall be appointed for a term of 4 years.\n**(3) Vacancies**\n**(A) In general**\nA vacancy in the Council shall be filled in the manner in which the original appointment was made and shall be subject to any conditions which applied with respect to the original appointment.\n**(B) Term**\nAn individual chosen to fill a vacancy shall be appointed for the unexpired term of the member replaced.\n**(C) Expiration**\nThe term of any member shall not expire before the date on which the member\u2019s successor takes office.\n##### (d) Majority approval\nThe Council shall act by resolution of a majority of the members.\n##### (e) Duties\nThe Council shall\u2014\n**(1)**\nadvise the Board and the Executive Director on matters relating to\u2014\n**(A)**\ninvestment policies for the Fund; and\n**(B)**\nthe administration of title I of this Act; and\n**(2)**\nperform such other duties as the Board may direct with respect to investment funds established in accordance with title I.\n#### 203. Executive Director\n##### (a) In general\n**(1) Appointment**\nThe Board shall appoint, without regard to the provisions of law governing appointments in the competitive service, an Executive Director by action agreed to by a majority of the members of the Board.\n**(2) Qualifications**\nThe Executive Director shall have substantial experience, training, and expertise in the management of financial investments and pension benefit plans.\n##### (b) Duties\nThe Executive Director shall\u2014\n**(1)**\ncarry out the policies established by the Board;\n**(2)**\ninvest and manage the Fund in accordance with investment policies and other policies established by the Board;\n**(3)**\nadminister the provisions of this Act;\n**(4)**\nprescribe such regulations (other than regulations relating to fiduciary responsibilities) as may be necessary for the administration of this Act;\n**(5)**\nmeet from time to time with the Council upon the request of the Council; and\n**(6)**\nenforce the financial literary requirements established by the Board pursuant to 201(f)(5).\n##### (c) Authorities\nThe Executive Director may\u2014\n**(1)**\nprescribe such regulations as may be necessary to carry out the responsibilities of the Executive Director under this section, other than regulations relating to fiduciary responsibilities;\n**(2)**\nappoint such personnel as may be necessary to carry out the provisions of this Act;\n**(3)**\nsubject to approval by the Board, procure the services of experts and consultants under section 3109 of title 5, United States Code;\n**(4)**\nmake such payments out of sums in the Fund as the Executive Director determines are necessary to carry out the provisions of this Act and the policies of the Board;\n**(5)**\npay the compensation, per diem, and travel expenses of individuals appointed under paragraphs (2), (3), and (7) of this subsection from the Fund;\n**(6)**\nexcept as otherwise expressly prohibited by law or the policies of the Board, delegate any of the Executive Director\u2019s functions to such employees under the Board as the Executive Director may designate and authorize such successive redelegations of such functions to such employees under the Board as the Executive Director may consider to be necessary or appropriate; and\n**(7)**\ntake such other actions as are appropriate to carry out the functions of the Executive Director.\n#### 204. Investment policies and selection of asset managers\n##### (a) Investment Policies\nThe Board shall develop investment policies under section 201(f)(1) which provide for\u2014\n**(1)**\nprudent investments suitable for accumulating funds for payment of retirement income; and\n**(2)**\nlow administrative costs.\n##### (b) Asset managers\nThe Board shall select asset managers to manage the Fund, subject to the following conditions:\n**(1)**\nThe Board shall select a number of asset managers necessary to ensure that no asset manager shall be responsible for managing the greater of\u2014\n**(A)**\n$500,000,000,000; or\n**(B)**\n10 percent of the Fund\u2019s assets.\n**(2)**\nThe Board shall limit any contract with an asset manager to a maximum of 5 years.\n#### 205. Administrative provisions\n##### (a) Board meetings\nThe Board shall meet\u2014\n**(1)**\nnot less than once during each month; and\n**(2)**\nat additional times at the call of the Chair.\n##### (b) Board governance\n**(1) In general**\nExcept as provided in section 201(g)(1)(C), the Board shall perform the functions and exercise the powers of the Board on a majority vote of a quorum of the Board.\n**(2) Quorum**\n3 members of the Board shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business.\n**(3) Effect of vacancy**\nA vacancy on the Board shall not impair the authority of a quorum of the Board to perform the functions and exercise the power of the Board.\n##### (c) Board compensation\n**(1) In general**\nEach member of the Board shall be compensated at the daily rate of basic pay for level IV of the Executive Schedule for each day during which such member is engaged in performing a function of the Board.\n**(2) Per diem, etc**\nA member of the Board shall be paid travel, per diem, and other necessary expenses while traveling away from such member\u2019s home or regular place of business in the performance of the duties of the Board.\n**(3) Payment from fund**\nPayments authorized under this subsection shall be paid from the Fund as administrative expenses permitted under section 101(e).\n#### 206. Fiduciary responsibilities; liability and penalties\n##### (a) Definitions\nFor the purposes of this section:\n**(1) Account**\nThe term account is not limited by the definition provided in section 2.\n**(2) Adequate consideration**\nThe term adequate consideration means\u2014\n**(A)**\nin the case of a security for which there is a generally recognized market\u2014\n**(i)**\nthe price of the security prevailing on a national securities exchange which is registered under section 6 of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ( 15 U.S.C. 78f ); or\n**(ii)**\nif the security is not traded on such a national securities exchange, a price not less favorable to the Fund than the offering price for the security as established by the current bid and asked prices quoted by persons independent of the issuer and of any party in interest; and\n**(B)**\nin the case of an asset other than a security for which there is a generally recognized market, the fair market value of the asset as determined in good faith by a fiduciary or fiduciaries in accordance with regulations prescribed by the Secretary of Labor.\n**(3) Fiduciary**\nThe term fiduciary means\u2014\n**(A)**\na member of the Board;\n**(B)**\nthe Executive Director;\n**(C)**\nany person who has or exercises discretionary authority or discretionary control over the management or disposition of the assets of the Fund; and\n**(D)**\nany person who, with respect to the Fund, is described in section 3(21)(A) of the Employee Retirement Income Security Act of 1974 ( 29 U.S.C. 1002(21)(A) ).\n**(4) Party in interest**\nThe term party in interest includes\u2014\n**(A)**\nany fiduciary;\n**(B)**\nany counsel to a person who is a fiduciary, with respect to the actions of such person as a fiduciary;\n**(C)**\nany participant;\n**(D)**\nany person providing services to the Board and, with respect to the actions of the Executive Director as a fiduciary, any person providing services to the Executive Director;\n**(E)**\na labor organization, the members of which are participants;\n**(F)**\na spouse, sibling, ancestor, lineal descendant, or spouse of a lineal descendant of a person described in subparagraph (A), (B), or (D);\n**(G)**\na corporation, partnership, or trust or estate of which, or in which, at least 50 percent of\u2014\n**(i)**\nthe combined voting power of all classes of stock entitled to vote or the total value of shares of all classes of stock of such corporation,\n**(ii)**\nthe capital interest or profits interest of such partnership, or\n**(iii)**\nthe beneficial interest of such trust or estate,\nis owned directly or indirectly or held by a person described in subparagraph (A), (B), (D), or (E);\n**(H)**\nan official (including a director) of, or an individual employed by, a person described in subparagraph (A), (B), (D), (E), or (G), or an individual having powers or responsibilities similar to those of such an official;\n**(I)**\na holder (directly or indirectly) of at least 10 percent of the shares in a person described in any subparagraph referred to in subparagraph (H); and\n**(J)**\na person who, directly or indirectly, is at least a 10 percent partner or joint venturer (measured in capital or profits) in a person described in any subparagraph referred to in subparagraph (H).\n##### (b) Duties\nTo the extent not inconsistent with the provisions of this Act and the policies prescribed by the Board, a fiduciary shall discharge the fiduciary\u2019s responsibilities with respect to the Fund or applicable portion thereof solely in the interest of the participants and beneficiaries and\u2014\n**(1)**\nfor the exclusive purpose of\u2014\n**(A)**\nproviding benefits to participants and their beneficiaries; and\n**(B)**\ndefraying reasonable expenses of administering the Fund or applicable portions thereof;\n**(2)**\nwith the care, skill, prudence, and diligence under the circumstances then prevailing that a prudent individual acting in a like capacity and familiar with such matters would use in the conduct of an enterprise of a like character and with like objectives; and\n**(3)**\nto the extent permitted by section 102, by diversifying the investments of the Fund or applicable portions thereof so as to minimize the risk of large losses, unless under the circumstances it is clearly prudent not to do so.\n##### (c) Ownership jurisdictions\nNo fiduciary may maintain the indicia of ownership of any assets of the Fund outside the jurisdiction of the district courts of the United States.\n##### (d) Transactions\n**(1) Prohibited transactions**\nA fiduciary shall not permit the Fund to engage in any of the following transactions, except in exchange for adequate consideration:\n**(A)**\nA transfer of any assets of the Fund to any person the fiduciary knows or should know to be a party in interest or the use of such assets by any such persons.\n**(B)**\nAn acquisition of any property from or sale of any property to the Fund by any person the fiduciary knows or should know to be a party in interest.\n**(C)**\nA transfer or exchange of services between the Fund and any person the fiduciary knows or should know to be a party in interest.\n**(2) Prohibited actions**\nNotwithstanding paragraph (1), a fiduciary with respect to the Fund shall not\u2014\n**(A)**\ndeal with any assets of the Fund in the fiduciary\u2019s own interest or for the fiduciary\u2019s own account;\n**(B)**\nact, in an individual capacity or any other capacity, in any transaction involving the Fund on behalf of a party, or representing a party, whose interests are adverse to the interests of the Fund or the interests of its participants or beneficiaries; or\n**(C)**\nreceive any consideration of the fiduciary\u2019s own personal account from any party dealing with sums credited to the Fund in connection with a transaction involving assets of the Fund.\n**(3) Secretary of Labor**\n**(A) In general**\nThe Secretary of Labor may, in accordance with procedures which the Secretary of Labor shall by regulation prescribe, grant a conditional or unconditional exemption of any fiduciary or transaction, or class of fiduciaries or transactions, from all or any of the restrictions imposed by paragraph (2). An exemption granted under this subparagraph shall not relieve a fiduciary from any other applicable provision of this Act.\n**(B) Conditions**\nThe Secretary of Labor may not grant an exemption under subparagraph (A) unless the Secretary of Labor finds that such exemption is\u2014\n**(i)**\nadministratively feasible;\n**(ii)**\nin the interests of the Fund and its participants; and\n**(iii)**\nprotective of the rights of participants and beneficiaries of such Fund.\n**(C) Notice**\nAn exemption under subparagraph (A) may not be granted unless\u2014\n**(i)**\nnotice of the proposed exemption is published in the Federal Register;\n**(ii)**\ninterested persons are given an opportunity to present views; and\n**(iii)**\nthe Secretary of Labor affords an opportunity for a hearing and makes a determination on the record with respect to the respective requirements of clauses (i), (ii), and (iii) of subparagraph (B).\n**(D) Application of ERISA fiduciary exemptions**\nNotwithstanding subparagraph (C), the Secretary of Labor may determine that an exemption granted for any class of fiduciaries or transactions under section 408(a) of the Employee Retirement Income Security Act of 1974 ( 29 U.S.C. 1108(a) ) shall, upon publication of notice in the Federal Register under this subparagraph, constitute an exemption from the application of paragraph (2).\n##### (e) Nonapplication\nThis section does not prohibit any fiduciary from\u2014\n**(1)**\nreceiving any benefit which the fiduciary is entitled to receive under this Act as a participant, former participant, or beneficiary;\n**(2)**\nreceiving any reasonable compensation authorized by this Act for services rendered, or for reimbursement of expenses properly and actually incurred, in the performance of the fiduciary\u2019s duties under this Act; or\n**(3)**\nserving as a fiduciary in addition to being an officer, employee, agent, or other representative of a party in interest.\n##### (f) Liability\n**(1) In general**\nAny fiduciary that breaches the responsibilities, duties, and obligations set out in subsection (b) or violates subsection (c) shall be personally liable to the Fund for any losses to such Fund resulting from each such breach or violation and to restore to such Fund any profits made by the fiduciary through use of assets of such Fund by the fiduciary, and, except as provided in paragraphs (3) and (4), shall be subject to such other equitable or remedial relief as a court considers appropriate. A fiduciary may be removed for a breach referred to in the preceding sentence.\n**(2) Civil penalties**\nThe Secretary of Labor may assess a civil penalty against a party in interest with respect to each transaction prohibited by subsection (d) which is engaged in by the party in interest. The amount of such penalty shall be equal to 5 percent of the amount involved in each such transaction (as defined in section 4975(f)(4) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986) for each year or part thereof during which the prohibited transaction continues, except that, if the transaction is not corrected (in such manner as the Secretary of Labor shall prescribe by regulation consistent with section 4975(f)(5) of such Code) within 90 days after the date the Secretary of Labor transmits notice to the party in interest (or such longer period as the Secretary of Labor may permit), such penalty may be in the amount of not more than 100 percent of the amount involved.\n**(3) Special rules**\n**(A) In general**\nA fiduciary shall not be liable under paragraph (1)\u2014\n**(i)**\nwith respect to a breach of fiduciary duty under subsection (b) committed before becoming a fiduciary or after ceasing to be a fiduciary;\n**(ii)**\nfor providing for the automatic enrollment of a participant in accordance with section 104;\n**(iii)**\nfor enrolling a participant or beneficiary in a default investment fund or option in accordance with section 104; or\n**(iv)**\nfor allowing a participant or beneficiary to invest through the mutual fund window or for establishing restrictions applicable to participants\u2019 or beneficiaries\u2019 ability to invest through the mutual fund window.\n**(B) Joint and several liability**\nA fiduciary shall be jointly and severally liable under paragraph (1) for a breach of fiduciary duty under subsection (b) by another fiduciary only if\u2014\n**(i)**\nthe fiduciary participates knowingly in, or knowingly undertakes to conceal, an act or omission of such other fiduciary, knowing such act or omission is such a breach;\n**(ii)**\nby the fiduciary\u2019s failure to comply with subsection (b) in the administration of the fiduciary\u2019s specific responsibilities which give rise to the fiduciary status, the fiduciary has enabled such other fiduciary to commit such a breach;
