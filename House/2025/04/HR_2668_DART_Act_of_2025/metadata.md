Short title\nThis Act may be cited as the Diversion And Rehabilitation Transformation Act of 2025 or the DART Act of 2025 .\n#### 2. Findings\nThe Congress finds the following:\n**(1)**\nDrug addiction, mental health disorders, and systemic poverty are significant contributing factors to crime rates across the United States.\n**(2)**\nTraditional criminal justice approaches, particularly incarceration, often fail to address the underlying causes of criminal behavior and can result in negative collateral consequences for individuals, families, and communities, including\u2014\n**(A)**\nloss of employment and housing;\n**(B)**\ndisruption of family relationships;\n**(C)**\nincreased risk of future criminal behavior; and\n**(D)**\nstrain on State and local resources.\n**(3)**\nPre-incarceration diversion and rehabilitation programs offer a cost-effective and evidence-based alternative to incarceration by connecting individuals with appropriate treatment, life-skills training, support services, and increasing connectivity to behavioral health services, thereby addressing the root causes of crime and promoting long-term public safety.\n**(4)**\nMany States and local jurisdictions, including St. Louis County, Missouri, have successfully implemented diversion and rehabilitation programs, demonstrating their effectiveness in reducing recidivism and improving community outcomes.\n**(5)**\nThe Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program (JAG) is a critical source of Federal funding for State and local criminal justice initiatives.\n**(6)**\nExpanding the allowable uses of JAG funds to specifically include pre-arrest diversion, specialty courts, and post-release rehabilitation will enhance the effectiveness of the JAG program and promote a more comprehensive approach to criminal justice reform.\n**(7)**\nThe implementation of evidence-based and trauma-informed practices within diversion and rehabilitation programs is essential for ensuring positive outcomes and reducing recidivism rates.\n#### 3. Purposes\nThe purposes of this Act are as follows:\n**(1)**\nTo reduce crime and recidivism rates nationwide by supporting the expansion and enhancement of pre-arrest diversion, court-based intervention, and post-release rehabilitation at the State and local levels.\n**(2)**\nTo promote a more effective and equitable criminal justice system that addresses the underlying causes of crime and provides opportunities for individuals to turn their lives around.\n**(3)**\nTo minimize the negative collateral consequences associated with incarceration and the criminal justice process.\n**(4)**\nTo encourage the adoption of evidence-based and trauma-informed practices in diversion and rehabilitation programs.\n**(5)**\nTo leverage the existing infrastructure of the Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program to efficiently and effectively support diversion and rehabilitation efforts.\n**(6)**\nTo prioritize funding for programs that incorporate mental health services, peer support, and restorative justice practices.\n#### 4. Expansion of program purpose areas under the Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program\nSection 501(a)(1) of the Omnibus Crime Control and Safe Streets Act of 1968 ( 34 U.S.C. 10152(a)(1) ) is amended\u2014\n**(1)**\nin subparagraph (H)\u2014\n**(A)**\nby striking and corrections programs and inserting , corrections, and diversion and rehabilitation programs ; and\n**(B)**\nby inserting before the period at the end the following: implemented at any phase of the criminal justice process (including pre-arrest and pre-trial) ; and\n**(2)**\nby adding at the end the following:\n(I) Specialty courts and judicial intervention programs, including restorative justice programs focusing on victim-involvement or community-based resolutions. .\n#### 5. National Diversion and Rehabilitation Clearinghouse\n##### (a) Establishment\nThe Attorney General is authorized to establish a National Diversion and Rehabilitation Clearinghouse to centralize information on evidence-based practices related to diversion and rehabilitation programs.\n##### (b) Functions\nThe Clearinghouse established under subsection (a) is authorized to\u2014\n**(1)**\ncollect and disseminate research on effective diversion and rehabilitation programs;\n**(2)**\nprovide technical assistance to States, local governments, and non-profits related to implementing a diversion and rehabilitation program;\n**(3)**\nconduct research and develop training materials on diversion and rehabilitation programs;\n**(4)**\nidentify and promote best practices for trauma-informed and restorative justice approaches to implementing a diversion and rehabilitation program; and\n**(B)**\nreduces an individual\u2019s involvement in the criminal justice system in both the short and long term.\n**(2)**\nThe term trauma-informed practice means, with respect to criminal justice and rehabilitation, an approach that recognizes the impact of trauma on individuals and seeks to create safe, supportive, and empowering environments that promote healing and reduce re-traumatization.\n**(3)**\nThe term evidence-based practice means a program or practice that\u2014\n**(A)**\nis demonstrated to be effective when implemented with fidelity;\n**(B)**\nis based on a clearly articulated and empirically supported theory;\n**(C)**\nhas measurable outcomes relevant to reducing incarceration;
