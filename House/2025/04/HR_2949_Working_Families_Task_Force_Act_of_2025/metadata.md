and\n**(2)**\ndevelop recommendations to improve the standard of living and quality of life for working families.\n##### (e) Duties\nThe duties of the Task Force shall include the following:\n**(1)**\nIdentifying and evaluating key factors that impact the standard of living for working families, including\u2014\n**(A)**\naddressing affordability challenges related to various economic conditions, including inflation;\n**(B)**\nreducing barriers to economic mobility;\n**(C)**\nimproving access to quality jobs with livable wages and strong labor standards;\n**(D)**\nexpanding affordable child care for all families;\n**(E)**\nimproving incentives, including tax policies such as the child tax credit, child and dependent care tax credit, and earned income tax credit, that assist children and families;\n**(F)**\nsupporting home care and medical care for seniors and families that need assistance;\n**(G)**\nmitigating barriers to more affordable and higher quality health care and services;\n**(H)**\nobtaining and building quality, affordable housing;\n**(I)**\nexpanding educational and workforce training opportunities;\n**(J)**\nincreasing financial literacy and access to financial services;\n**(K)**\naccessing affordable and nutritious food;\n**(L)**\nreducing gaps in access to technology and the internet that impact educational and employment opportunities and health services;\n**(M)**\nreducing environmental hazards and creating a healthier environment;\n**(N)**\naccessing affordable and renewable energy sources;\n**(O)**\naccessing affordable, reliant, and efficient transportation systems; and\n**(P)**\naddressing the impacts of staffing and funding cuts at Federal agencies.\n**(2)**\nAssessing or developing a methodology to assess how various macroeconomic conditions (which may include unemployment, inflation, gross domestic product, and monetary policy) affect quality of life and affordability for working families.\n**(3)**\nAssessing the effectiveness of current Federal policies and programs in helping working families achieve an improved standard of living.\n**(4)**\nDeveloping legislative and regulatory policy recommendations to enhance the efforts of Federal agencies and Congress to empower working families to meet the challenges of current economic conditions, raise their standards of living, and access the benefits of economic growth.\n**(5)**\nThe Task Force shall consult with a wide-ranging group of external stakeholders and public experts in order to develop the recommendations referred to in paragraph (4).\n##### (f) Report\nNot later than 180 days after the date of enactment of this Act, the Task Force shall\u2014\n**(1)**\nsubmit to the appropriate congressional committees a report on its most recent findings and recommendations, which shall include a list of stakeholders and public experts with which the Task Force consulted and the meeting minutes for each meeting of the Task Force;
