Program establishment\n##### (a) Establishment\nThe Director shall establish and maintain a habitat connectivity mapping and science program to carry out this section.\n##### (b) Purpose\nThe purpose of the program is to develop mapping and science to support Federal, State, local, and Tribal governments and the public in efforts to protect habitat connectivity.\n##### (c) Wildlife movement and habitat connectivity mapping\n**(1) Mapping efforts**\nThe Director shall expand and build upon existing agency efforts to map habitat connectivity for native species. Such efforts shall include working with, incorporating data from, and sharing data between\u2014\n**(A)**\nFederal agencies;\n**(B)**\nState, Tribal, and local governments;\n**(C)**\nnongovernmental organizations; and\n**(D)**\nacademic institutions.\n**(2) Use of technology**\nThe Director shall\u2014\n**(A)**\nuse new data management, visualization, and analysis capabilities to analyze wildlife movements and delineate habitat connectivity; and\n**(B)**\nshare these capabilities with Federal, State, local, and Tribal wildlife managers.\n**(3) Distribution of maps**\nThe Director shall develop and make available to the public\u2014\n**(A)**\na database of corridors and habitat connectivity; and\n**(B)**\nmaps of habitat connectivity and National Wildlife Corridors to guide land-use planning and on-the-ground management of native species.\n**(4) Report**\nNot later than 2 years after the date of the enactment of this section, the Director shall submit to the Committee on Natural Resources of the House of Representatives and the Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate a report that\u2014\n**(A)**\nprovides an assessment of existing maps, data, models, surveys, and descriptions of habitat connectivity that have been developed by\u2014\n**(i)**\nFederal, State, Tribal, and local fish and wildlife agencies and natural heritage programs;\n**(ii)**\nnongovernmental organizations; and\n**(iii)**\nindustry; and\n**(B)**\nidentifies gaps in information about corridors and habitat connectivity.\n**(5) Proprietary interests and protected information**\nIn carrying out this section, the Director shall\u2014\n**(A)**\nprotect proprietary interests with respect to confidential information and licensed data; and\n**(B)**\nprotect such information relating to the habitats and ranges of specific native fish and wildlife as the Director determines appropriate to protect such fish and wildlife.\n##### (d) Wildlife movement and habitat connectivity research\nThe Director shall conduct research to help guide management of National Wildlife Corridors, including\u2014\n**(1)**\na study to evaluate the impacts of climate change on habitat connectivity and forecast how National Wildlife Corridors may need to shift to allow native species to adapt;\n**(2)**\na study to quantify the effect of ongoing and planned development on habitat connectivity and National Wildlife Corridors;\n**(3)**\na study to develop new mapping methods to prioritize critical corridors and habitat connectivity areas; and\n**(4)**\na study to evaluate the effectiveness of efforts to address habitat fragmentation and mitigation barriers to habitat connectivity.\n##### (e) Definitions\nIn this section:\n**(1) Director**\nThe term Director means the Director of the United States Geological Survey.\n**(2) Program**\nThe term program means the habitat connectivity mapping and science program established by the Director under subsection (a).\nII\nCOLLABORATION, PARTNERSHIP, AND CONSERVATION OF AMERICAN WILDLIFE CORRIDORS\n#### 201. Establishment of National Wildlife Corridors System on Federal land and water\n##### (a) Establishment\nThere is established a system of corridors on Federal land and water, to be known as the National Wildlife Corridor System , which shall consist of National Wildlife Corridors designated as part of the National Wildlife Corridor System by\u2014\n**(1)**\nstatute;\n**(2)**\nrulemaking; or\n**(3)**\na land use management plan developed or revised by the Secretaries.\n##### (b) Strategy\nNot later than 18 months after the date of the enactment of this section, the Secretary shall develop a strategy for the effective development of the National Wildlife Corridor System\u2014\n**(1)**\nto support the fulfillment of the purposes described in section 2(b);\n**(2)**\nto ensure coordination and consistency across Federal agencies in the development, implementation, and management of National Wildlife Corridors; and\n**(3)**\nto develop a timeline for the implementation of National Wildlife Corridors.\n#### 202. Nominations of National Wildlife Corridors and prioritization criteria\n##### (a) Partner nominations\nState, Tribal, and local governments, non-governmental organizations, and collaborative partnerships may nominate Federal lands and waters as National Wildlife Corridors.\n##### (b) Coordinating Committee nominations\nThe Coordinating Committee shall, not later than 2 years after the date on which the Coordinating Committee is established and not less frequently than every 5 years thereafter, nominate habitat connectivity areas to be designated as National Wildlife Corridors.\n##### (c) Nominated areas\nWith respect to any area for which the Secretary concerned receives a nomination under subsection (a) or (b), the Secretary concerned shall make a determination not later than 1 year after the date on which the Secretary concerned receives such nomination.\n##### (d) Prioritization criteria\nThe Secretaries shall establish criteria to determine which habitat connectivity areas to designate as National Wildlife Corridors. Such criteria shall include\u2014\n**(1)**\nsupport from the best available science relating to\u2014\n**(A)**\nexisting habitat connectivity; and\n**(B)**\npotential future habitat connectivity;\n**(2)**\nwhether the Federal land or water merits inclusion within the National Wildlife Corridor System because it\u2014\n**(A)**\nprovides habitat connectivity and supports wildlife movement;\n**(B)**\nhas the potential to benefit more than 1 species of fish and wildlife; or\n**(C)**\nhas the potential to benefit a fish or wildlife species that is listed as a threatened species or an endangered species under the Endangered Species Act of 1973 ( 16 U.S.C. 1531 et seq. ); and\n**(3)**\nthe consideration of recommendations from the Coordinating Committee under section 205(c).\n#### 203. Management of National Wildlife Corridors\n##### (a) In general\nThe Secretaries shall manage National Wildlife Corridors in a manner that contributes to the long-term persistence, resilience, adaptability, and movement of native species through\u2014\n**(1)**\nthe maintenance, restoration, or improvement of habitat connectivity, including establishment of goals and monitoring plans;\n**(2)**\nthe prohibition of human infrastructure, development, and activities that impede wildlife movement, except where required to meet the minimum requirements for the purposes of this Act;\n**(3)**\nthe implementation of strategies and activities that enhance the ability of native species to adapt to changing environmental conditions;\n**(4)**\nthe use of existing conservation programs managed by the Secretary concerned to contribute to the persistence, resilience, adaptability, and wildlife movement of native species;
