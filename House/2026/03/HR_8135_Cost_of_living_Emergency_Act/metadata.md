and\n**(4)**\nthe Committee on Energy and Commerce of the House of Representatives.\n##### (g) Termination\nThe Task Force shall terminate on the date that is 30 days after the expiration or rescission of the emergency declared under section 3.\n##### (h) Rule of Construction\nNothing in this Act shall be construed as authorizing the Department of Justice or the Federal Trade Commission to suspend, terminate, or deprioritize any ongoing investigation, enforcement action, or litigation initiated by the Task Force that is ongoing at the time the Task Force terminates pursuant to subsection (g).\n#### 7. Use of authorities under Defense Production Act of 1950\n##### (a) Covered agency head defined\nIn this section, the term covered agency head means the head of an agency to whom the President delegated, under section 201(a) of Executive Order 13603 (77 Fed. and\n**(B)**\ncan be reasonably proven to result in a reduction of the retail or wholesale cost of a specific basic household necessity within 180 days of the commencement of the activity.\n**(4) Authorized actions**\nIn carrying out subsection (a), the President may carry out the following actions:\n**(A) Loan guarantees and loans**\nIssue guarantees and make loans under sections 301 and 302 of the Defense Production Act of 1950 ( 50 U.S.C. 4531 , 4532) to small- and medium-sized businesses to modernize facilities for the production of basic household necessities.\n**(B) Purchase commitments**\nExecute purchase commitments under section 303 of the Defense Production Act of 1950 ( 50 U.S.C. 4533 ) to make available basic household necessities at a consistent price to individuals in the United States for the duration of the agreement.\n**(C) Production cost offset**\nProvide for subsidy payments under section 303(c) of the Defense Production Act of 1950 ( 50 U.S.C. 4533(c) ) to offset the costs of domestically produced raw materials essential to the production of basic household necessities.\n##### (d) Exemption from national defense requirement\nUnder subsections (b) and (c), the President shall exercise the authorities provided under title III of the Defense Production Act of 1950 ( 50 U.S.C. 4531 et seq. ) without regard to any requirement of that Act that the exercise of such authorities be for the national defense.\n#### 8. Congressional action on cost-of-living emergency\n##### (a) Establishment of Cost-of-Living Commission\n**(1) Establishment**\nThere is hereby established in Congress a Cost-of-Living Commission.\n**(2) Duties**\n**(A) General**\nThe Commission shall identify policies to\u2014\n**(i)**\nmeaningfully improve the cost-of-living situation for the average U.S. household in the short-
