Short title\nThis Act may be cited as the Reproductive Healthcare Leave Act .\n#### 2. Purposes\nThe purpose of this Act is to allow individuals in the United States to receive paid leave time for absences resulting from a physical or mental conditions or from symptoms related to reproductive health or for absences related to obtaining care related to reproductive health, and for other purposes.\n#### 3. Paid leave time\n##### (a) Granting leave time\n**(1) In general**\nAn employer shall grant to each employee employed by the employer 96 hours of paid leave time on the employee's first workday of each calendar year. The employee may use the paid leave time as needed during that calendar year for reasons described in subsection (b).\n**(2) Carryover**\nPaid leave time granted under this section shall not carry over from 1 calendar year to the next.\n**(3) Employers with existing policies**\nAny employer with a paid leave policy who makes available an amount of paid leave that is sufficient to meet the requirements of this section and that is made available for all stated reasons and under all stated conditions that are the same as the reasons and conditions outlined in subsection (b) shall not be required to grant an employee additional paid leave time under this section.\n**(4) Construction**\nNothing in this section shall be construed as requiring financial or other reimbursement to an employee from an employer upon the employee\u2019s termination, resignation, retirement, or other separation from employment for paid leave time granted under paragraph (1) that has not been used.\n**(5) Prohibition**\nAn employer may not require, as a condition of providing paid leave time under this section, that the employee involved search for or find a replacement employee to cover the hours during which the employee is using paid leave time.\n##### (b) Uses\nPaid leave time granted under this section may be used by an employee for any of the following reasons:\n**(1)**\nA medical or surgical procedure related to human reproductive health, including any physical or mental symptom related to such a procedure or any reproductive health condition, including\u2014\n**(A)**\nMenstruation.\n**(B)**\nEndometriosis.\n**(C)**\nDysmenorrhea.\n**(D)**\nAdenomyosis.\n**(E)**\nOlycystic ovary syndrome.\n**(F)**\nMenopause.\n**(G)**\nPerimenopause.\n**(2)**\nObtaining medical care, have a medical procedure, or a preventative screening related to reproductive health, including fertility treatments, termination of pregnancies, hysterectomies, and vasectomies.\n##### (c) Procedures\nAn employer shall allow paid leave time to be used upon the oral or written request of an employee. Such a request shall\u2014\n**(1)**\ninclude the expected duration of the period of such time; and\n**(2)**\nbe provided as soon as practicable after the employee is aware of the need for such period.\n#### 4.
