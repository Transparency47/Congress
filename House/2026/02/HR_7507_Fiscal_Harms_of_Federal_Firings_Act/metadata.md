Short title\nThis Act may be cited as the Fiscal Harms of Federal Firings Act .\n#### 2. Findings\nCongress finds the following:\n**(1)**\nFederal Reductions in Force (RIFs) may displace workers, altering where affected individuals live and work across States and localities.\n**(2)**\nSuch workforce reductions may increase demand for State-administered services, including unemployment insurance, medicaid, workforce retraining, housing assistance, and other social services.\n**(3)**\nRIFs may reduce economic activity and tax revenues in regions with a high concentration of Federal employment.\n**(4)**\nStates and local governments vary significantly in their fiscal capacity to absorb the short- and long-term impacts of large-scale Federal workforce reductions.\n**(5)**\nThere is currently no comprehensive Federal assessment of how RIFs affect State and local government budgets, revenues, and expenditures.\n#### 3. and\n**(C)**\nState and local government fiscal capacity and labor market conditions;\n**(6)**\nhistorical case studies of significant RIFs on States or local governments occurring within the previous 20 years; and\n**(7)**\nstrategies used by States and local governments to mitigate fiscal and economic impacts.\n##### (c) Consultation and data collection\n**(1) Consultation**\nIn conducting the study, the Comptroller General shall seek to consult with\u2014\n**(A)**\nState and local government budget officers and governors offices;\n**(B)**\nState and local government workforce and labor agencies;\n**(C)**\nrelevant Federal agencies, including the Office of Personnel Management and the Department of Labor; and\n**(D)**\neconomists, labor market experts, and public finance researchers.\n**(2) Data sources**\nThe study may use administrative data, surveys, economic modeling, and publicly available fiscal and labor statistics.\n##### (d) GAO reports to Congress\n**(1) Deadline**\nNot later than 18 months after the date of the enactment of this Act, the Comptroller General shall submit a report on the study to\u2014\n**(A)**\nthe Committee on Education and Workforce of the House of Representatives;\n**(B)**\nthe Committee on Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions of the Senate;\n**(C)**\nthe Committee on Oversight and Government Reform of the House of Representatives; and\n**(D)**\nthe Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs of the Senate.\n**(2) Contents**\nThe report under paragraph (1) shall include\u2014\n**(A)**\nfindings and analysis from the study, including\u2014\n**(i)**\nan analysis of whether each RIF studied improved the respective agency\u2019s efficiency; and\n**(ii)**\nany recommendations, including any recommendations for statutory or administrative changes to provide support for displaced employees and States and local governments impacted by RIFs;\n**(B)**\nidentification of States and local governments that experienced the greatest fiscal effects of RIFs;\n**(C)**\nprojected short-term and long-term budgetary impacts on States and local governments; and\n**(D)**\npolicy options for Congress to consider, including potential Federal assistance, coordination mechanisms, or advance planning tools.\n**(3) Public availability**\nThe Comptroller General shall make the report under paragraph (1) publicly available on the website of the Government Accountability Office.\n##### (e) Definitions\nIn this Act\u2014\n**(1)**\nthe term reduction in force or RIF means a reduction in force under subchapter I of chapter 35 or section 3595 of title 5, United States Code, or any other significant downsizing of Federal civilian employees;
